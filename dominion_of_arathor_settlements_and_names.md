# The Dominion of Arathor

## Overview

**The Dominion of Arathor** is a proud, militaristic, and disciplined human nation. Its society is structured around hierarchy, honor, duty, martial service, and ancestral achievement.

Arathorian culture carries a strong sense of authority and dignity. Its naming style and settlement names are influenced by a blend of **Latin**, **Germanic**, and **Old English** traditions, creating a tone that feels regal, classical, martial, and severe.

Arathor can be imagined as an imperial, legionary, fortress-minded realm: orderly, proud, and deeply invested in military strength.

---

## Arathorian Naming Style

Arathorian names reflect the proud, militaristic, and disciplined nature of the Dominion.

Influenced by the Dominion's structured society and martial heritage, these names carry an air of authority, dignity, and strength. They often have a regal or classical tone, combining elements of Latin, Germanic, and Old English traditions.

Surnames frequently denote military ranks, professions, or ancestral achievements, emphasizing devotion to hierarchy and honor.

### Naming Style Notes

- **First names** are formal and commanding, often Romanesque or noble in sound.
- **Male names** tend to sound bold, decisive, and authoritative.
- **Female names** are elegant but strong, reflecting the Dominion's respect for discipline and leadership.
- **Surnames** are often tied to military roles, ancestral accomplishments, noble houses, or martial virtues.
- **Titles and ranks** are commonly used in introductions, such as:
  - General Marcus Tyralon
  - Dame Selene Vaelora
  - Captain Darius Thalric
  - Marshal Aurelia Fendralis

Status and accomplishments are culturally important, so titles establish credibility and authority in conversation.

# Tone Comparison: Arathor and Verosia

## Verosia

Verosia's names and settlements tend to feel:

- Lyrical
- Courtly
- Arcane
- Forested
- Coastal
- Blended between Romance, Celtic, and Germanic influences
- More flexible and culturally mixed

## Arathor

Arathor's names and settlements tend to feel:

- Militaristic
- Imperial
- Disciplined
- Fortified
- Hierarchical
- Classical and Germanic
- Focused on duty, rank, honor, and strength

Where Verosia feels like a kingdom of courts, forests, magic, and trade, Arathor feels like a Dominion of roads, banners, forts, legions, and command.
