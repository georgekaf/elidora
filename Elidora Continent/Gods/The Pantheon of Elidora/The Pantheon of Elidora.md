



### **Pantheon of the Ten Faceless Divines**

This pantheon consists of gods that are worshiped without strict domains, embodying broad concepts and ideals rather than specific aspects of existence. They are venerated across Elidora by various cultures, each interpreting their purposes in unique ways.

---

#### **1. Caelirion, The Boundless**

- **Concept:** Infinite potential and freedom.
- **Symbol:** An open circle surrounded by stars.
- **Worshippers:** Dreamers, explorers, and revolutionaries.
- **Description:** Caelirion represents endless possibilities and the pursuit of personal and collective growth. Worshippers celebrate freedom of thought, expression, and the breaking of limitations.

---

#### **2. Ithyra, The Eternal Bloom**

- **Concept:** Growth, renewal, and the cycle of life.
- **Symbol:** A single blooming flower with roots twisting into infinity.
- **Worshippers:** Farmers, healers, and those who seek new beginnings.
- **Description:** Ithyra embodies the vitality of life and the resilience of nature. Worshipers honor her during harvests and moments of rebirth.

---

#### **3. Thalovar, The Unbroken**

- **Concept:** Strength, endurance, and perseverance.
- **Symbol:** A mountain peak with a crack running through it.
- **Worshippers:** Warriors, builders, and survivors.
- **Description:** Thalovar inspires those who endure hardship and rise stronger from it, symbolizing the triumph of will over adversity.

---

#### **4. Lyseris, The Shifting Tide**

- **Concept:** Change, adaptability, and uncertainty.
- **Symbol:** A wave frozen mid-crash.
- **Worshippers:** Sailors, merchants, and agents of change.
- **Description:** Lyseris is revered as the ever-shifting force of change, teaching mortals to adapt and flow with life’s uncertainties.

---

#### **5. Ashirn, The Keeper of Whispers**

- **Concept:** Secrets, shadows, and hidden truths.
- **Symbol:** A mask split into black and white halves.
- **Worshippers:** Spies, thieves, and those who value discretion.
- **Description:** Ashirn thrives in secrecy, representing the unseen forces that shape the world. Worshippers believe knowledge hidden is as powerful as knowledge revealed.

---

#### **6. Eryndra, The Flame Undying**

- **Concept:** Passion, creativity, and inspiration.
- **Symbol:** A flame rising from a quill.
- **Worshippers:** Artists, poets, and revolutionaries.
- **Description:** Eryndra inspires creation and emotion, kindling the flames of passion in those who seek to leave their mark on the world.

---

#### **7. Morvyn, The Veilwalker**

- **Concept:** Transition, death, and the unknown.
- **Symbol:** A shrouded figure holding a lantern.
- **Worshippers:** Undertakers, seers, and those who guide others through difficult transitions.
- **Description:** Morvyn embodies the crossing of thresholds, from life to death, and from ignorance to understanding. Followers view death as a journey, not an end.

---

#### **8. Zelthar, The Unfettered Flame**

- **Concept:** Chaos, ambition, and destruction.
- **Symbol:** A flame erupting from broken chains.
- **Worshippers:** Rebels, warriors, and those who reject authority.
- **Description:** Zelthar represents the destructive forces of chaos that pave the way for new beginnings. Worshippers seek to break down the old to build the new.

---

#### **9. Veyruna, The Silent Guardian**

- **Concept:** Protection, sacrifice, and loyalty.
- **Symbol:** A shield with an eye in its center.
- **Worshippers:** Guardians, defenders, and those who value selflessness.
- **Description:** Veyruna watches over those who protect others, embodying quiet strength and unwavering loyalty to one’s cause or people.

---

#### **10. Nytheris, The Dreamweaver**

- **Concept:** Dreams, imagination, and the subconscious.
- **Symbol:** A starry web stretching between two crescent moons.
- **Worshippers:** Dreamers, mystics, and those seeking inspiration.
- **Description:** Nytheris governs the realm of dreams and visions, inspiring mortals to see beyond reality and into realms of possibility.

### **Roleplaying Note**

These gods are not tied to specific alignments or rigid dogmas, allowing for flexible interpretation. Worshippers often venerate multiple deities depending on their needs or philosophies. The gods themselves are seen as enigmatic forces, less involved in mortal affairs than traditional pantheons.


All other Gods (Toril pantheon, Egyptian, Norse, Greek) are known as Unknown Deities, that have revealed themeselves to individuals in an attempt to gain more followers and power.
