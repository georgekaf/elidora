

The image shows exactly the kind of scar we discussed: a vast, flooded region of dead trees, scattered ruins, and warped terrain—a reminder of where the **Sphere of Oblivion** struck. Its unnatural coastline and strange energy lines mark the site as something _wrong_, a place not born of natural geography but carved out by magic’s violence.

### Characteristics of _The Wound_:

1. **Unstable Terrain** – Parts of the land sink beneath shallow, magical waters, while other patches of ground jut out unnaturally. Whole chunks of earth look misplaced, as though torn from another plane.
2. **Dead Forests** – The skeletal trees scattered across the water are remnants of thriving forests that were half-displaced. Their roots cling to soil that no longer belongs to this world, drained of life by the unstable energies.
3. **Arcane Residue** – The strange rippling rings across the water aren’t currents but magical disturbances—the boundary scars of the Sphere’s displacement. Sailors say you can hear whispers on the waves, voices of those who were torn away.
4. **The Curse of Memory** – Locals call it _The Wound_ because it feels alive, festering. Fishermen speak of catching things not of this world, twisted creatures birthed in the unstable magic-plane. Travelers often vanish if they stray too deep into its waters.
5. **Symbol of Blame** – For [[The Kingdom of Verosia]] and [[The Empire of Solari]], The Wound is a _shared shame_. For some, it is a place to be forgotten. For Arcturus, it is the **gravesite of his village and family**, the scar he carries in his heart.

**Survivors/Echoes of the Wound** can serve as both horror and tragedy, reinforcing Arcturus’s pain and the campaign’s themes. Here are a few categories of what could seep through the unstable veil between _The Wound_ and the “other place” it was displaced into:

---

### 1. **Echoed Survivors**

These are not truly alive, nor fully dead. They are warped imprints of people who were pulled into the unstable plane.

- **The Hollowed** – Ghost-like figures of villagers who endlessly repeat their last moments. They do not recognize the living and cannot be reasoned with, their voices echoing like broken records.
- **The Fractured** – Survivors who partially exist in two planes at once. They flicker, their bodies warped and incomplete. They suffer constantly, begging to be released but lashing out violently when approached.

---

### 2. **Planar Beasts**

Animals and wildlife displaced alongside humans became something new—unnatural predators.

- **Glass Hounds** – Once village dogs, their forms are now crystalline and translucent, reflecting multiple realities at once. They hunt in silence.
- **Mist Stags** – Deer warped by unstable magic, antlers dripping with liquid arcane energy. They appear as majestic guardians at first but dissolve into violent frenzies if disturbed.

---

### 3. **The Corrupted Land**

The plane itself bleeds into The Wound, manifesting horrors in the environment.

- **The Singing Waters** – Pools that hum with voices of those lost. Drinking from them shows glimpses of their last thoughts but causes madness.
- **The Weeping Trees** – Dead trees that occasionally animate, their bark forming the faces of lost villagers, crying resin like blood.
- **The Shifting Islets** – Chunks of land blink in and out of reality. Sometimes they carry buildings or corpses that shouldn’t exist anymore.

---

### 4. **The Nexus Remnants**

Because the Sphere of Oblivion feeds on **Nexus Souls**, fragments of that stolen power remain.

- **Soul Shards** – Glowing crystals occasionally surface from The Wound. Touching one grants visions of other planes, but slowly drains the life of the wielder.
- **Living Conduits** – Rarely, a warped survivor emerges who contains a fragment of Nexus energy. They are unstable but could be hunted by Solari, Verosia, or even Arcturus for their power.

---

### 5. **The Emotional Weight**

The greatest horror is not the monsters, but the tragedy. NPCs might encounter:

- A child’s toy floating where a village once stood.
- Voices in the mist calling by name, as if the dead still remember.
- Survivors of border towns who see The Wound as a curse and whisper that “the dead aren’t gone.”
---

This makes The Wound both a **setting for adventures** and a **symbol of trauma**. Arcturus could return here often, not because it holds power, but because it is his tether to what was taken.

Recommendation: Use The Wound as a **semi-living dungeon**—sometimes it spawns creatures, sometimes memories, sometimes relics.
