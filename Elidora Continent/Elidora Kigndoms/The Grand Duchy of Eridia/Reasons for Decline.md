## Structural cracks

- **Debt spiral:** Years of selling **timber and silver forward** to finance levees and border guards → crushing interest to foreign factors (some Solari-front). Cash today, sovereignty tomorrow.
    
- **Aging infrastructure:** **Levees, canals, and mills** need overhaul; deferred maintenance means **floods and silted channels** that choke trade.
    

## External pressure

- **Solari squeeze:** Price-fixing on mastwood, “security advisers” on the frontier, and covert meddling by **Delacroix & Thorne** redirect contracts and patrols to benefit Solari.
    
- **Trade route shifts:** Drakonia’s pilotage monopoly on Medeia and Orlais convoy routes **bypass Eridian docks** when storms rise or fees spike.
    

## Resource strain

- **Overcut quotas & poaching:** Tallheart oak stands are slower to recover; black-market logging (sometimes protected by bribed officers) undercuts legal sales.
    
- **Mine problems:** Water ingress in silver shafts, fewer rich veins, and rising costs for safe extraction.
    

## Governance & class issues

- **Court ossification:** High Houses cling to ceremony; **Council vetoes** stall reforms. Real decisions migrate to back rooms (and foreign salons).
    
- **Temple–Guild friction:** The **Oak Priory** guards tithe rights and land tenures; **guild cities** want audits and investment → policy paralysis.
    

## Human capital flight

- **Brain drain:** Glassmakers, luthiers, and young engineers **emigrate to Orlais/Verosia** for better pay and grants.
    
- **Demoralized levies:** Greenwardens are excellent—but thin on the ground, underpaid, and used to plug budget holes (timber escorts, river taxes).
    

## Arcane shortfall

- **Arcanic dependence:** Eridia has **purity silver** but few high-tier mages; buys enchantments at a premium. When Verosia tightened research and Solari hoarded, **Eridia got the scraps**.
    

---

## What this looks like to citizens

- Prices creep up; **coin feels lighter** each year.
    
- **Flood seasons** hit harder; the **Verdant Assizes** are busy with property disputes.
    
- Harbor towns see more **foreign flags** and fewer local barges.
    
- **Public pageantry stays grand** even as roads crumble—people notice.
    

---

## How to arrest the decline (policy levers you can use in play)

- **Levee & canal works** funded by a **transparent timber tithe** + Orlais credit (with public audits).
    
- **Mastwood moratorium** + **replanting guilds**; crush poaching with mixed Priory–Guild patrols.
    
- **Trade rebalancing:** Joint **pilotage treaty with Drakonia**; fixed-rate berth guarantees to Verosia.
    
- **Talent stay/return program:** Tax holidays, **artisan academies**, prize funds for optics/luthiers.
    
- **Cut the rot:** Expose Delacroix’s shell firms; court-martial Thorne’s cronies; rebuild a **nonpartisan River Guard**.
    
- **Arcane partnerships:** Sponsor Verosian journeymages and Zephyrite hedge-wizards in exchange for lab space & public works enchantments.

## Adventure hooks that hit these causes

1. **The Flood Ledger:** Prove where levee money went—raid a countinghouse that stores Delacroix’s off-book ledgers.
    
2. **Poachers’ Accord:** Infiltrate a Solari-backed logging camp wearing Eridian colors; flip the foreman and seize the papers.
    
3. **Optic Exodus:** An Orlais patron tries to steal an entire lens guild—escort, negotiate, or outbid.
    
4. **The Silt Cut:** Hold a canal-cut worksite against bandits and a bribed captain until the floodgate is finished.
    
5. **Pilot’s Pact:** Broker a three-way deal (Eridia–Drakonia–Verosia) at a storm-lashed lighthouse while a Solari courier spreads forged terms.
    

**Bottom line:** Eridia isn’t dying—it’s **bleeding slowly** from debt, neglect, and manipulation. Fix the water, the books, and the brain drain, and the stag can rise again.
