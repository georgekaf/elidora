# The Grand Duchy of Eridia — Socio-Economic & Political State

## Government (who actually rules)

- **Type:** **Autonomous Grand Duchy** of Elven noble houses; sovereignty by ancient charter but hemmed by debts and treaties.
    
- **Head of State:** **Grand Duke/Duchess** (hereditary), styles “Warden of the Green Crown.”
    
- **High Council of Boughs:** delegates from great houses, guild-princes, and the Oak Priory. Can veto ducal decrees with a **two-thirds “Falling Leaves” vote**.
    
- **Courts:** **Verdant Assizes** (itinerant judges) + **Oak Priory** (temple auditors) oversee forest law, river rights, and tithe disputes.
    

**Conspiracy nodes:**

- **Minister Delacroix** (Treasury/Trade): steering resource concessions toward Solari front companies.
    
- **Captain Thorne** (Frontier Command): shifting patrol routes to “lose” shipments and pressure border towns into pro-Solari contracts.
    

---

## Economy (once-great, now strained)

|Sector|Output|Why Eridia still matters|Current Problems|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Timber & Ship-Masts**|Tallheart oak, white elm spars, pitch|Best mastwood on the sea; naval buyers queue|Overcutting quotas, smuggling, Solari price-fixing|
|**Silver & “Moon-ore”**|Fine silver, low-impurity veins; traces of arcanic ore|Coinage quality; enchanters prize purity|Mine flooding; debt to foreign factors|
|**Glass & Luthier Arts**|Crystal, lenses, instruments|Prestige crafts exported to Orlais/Verosia|Skilled youth emigrating for better pay|
|**Herbal Lore & Tinctures**|Healing resins, rare fungi, perfumes|Temple gardens’ recipes guarded|Poachers, counterfeiters on Solari border|
|**River Trade & Docks**|Barge building, millworks|Short sea hops feed Verosian ports|Silted channels; neglected levees|

**Money & tax:** Silver **stagleaf**, copper **acorn**; wartime **“Crown Rings”** bonds sold to nobles/guilds. Tithes on timber & ore fund the court; city excises light to keep artisans home.

---

## Society (what life feels like)

- **Elven majority** with human/dwarven enclaves in craft quarters; mixed villages along rivers.
    
- **Strata:** High Houses → Lesser Houses → Guild Compacts → Freeholders → Forest Tenants. Social mobility exists through **craft mastery** or **temple service**.
    
- **Culture:** Formal, mannered, music-rich. Public disputations in garden cloisters. Even rural festivals are choreographed pageantry.
    
- **Faith:** **Oak Priory** venerates cycles of growth/decline; controls sanctuaries, seed-granaries, and oath-groves.
    

---

## Security & Forces

- **Greenwardens:** light infantry/archers, superb in woods and causeways.
    
- **River Guard:** barges with scorpions; levy from mill towns.
    
- **High Road Knights:** dwindling heavy cavalry attached to noble houses.
    
- **Doctrine:** Refuse pitched plains battles; **canal cuts, forest screens, ambush at bridges**.
    

---

## Politics (active blocs)

|Faction|What they want|Where they clash|
|---|---|---|
|**Restorationists** (old houses)|Raise tariffs, rebuild levees, keep Verosia friendly|Expensive program vs. empty treasury|
|**Accommodationists** (Delacroix’s circle)|Long concessions to Solari for ready cash|Loss of autonomy, forest overcutting|
|**Reform Guilders** (craft cities)|Charter rights, anti-corruption audits, invest in artisans|Priory wary of losing tithe authority|
|**Border Hawks** (Thorne’s admirers)|Militarize river forts, seize smugglers, hire Drakonian pilots|Risks war or embargo|

**Public mood:** Proud, anxious, allergic to open war. Emigration whispers grow louder each harvest missed.

## Diplomacy (quick chart)

|Neighbor|Official Stance|Reality|
|---|---|---|
|**Verosia**|Historic partner & sea-trader|Wants stable Eridian timber; quietly funds levees|
|**Solari**|“Necessary buyer”|Pushing monopolies via Delacroix; border pressure via Thorne|
|**Orlais**|Cultural cousin|Buys glass/instruments; offers credit with strings|
|**Drakonia**|Respectful neutral|Hired pilots/escorts; wary of Solari claws on Eridian ports|

## Current fault lines & hooks

1. **The Mast-Right Auction:** Delacroix advances a 10-year Solari concession; guilders demand a public tender—sabotage and bribery abound.
    
2. **Flood on the Twin Peaks Run:** Levees fail; PCs lead relief and expose why the budget vanished (bonds → shell firms).
    
3. **The Smothered Stag:** Border manor torched; Thorne blames “Eldorian rebels.” Tracks say otherwise.
    
4. **Glassmaker’s Strike:** Optic lens guild halts work over tax seizures; Verosia offers to relocate them—save the guild or watch the brain-drain.
    
5. **Priory Ledger:** An oak priest offers PCs access to forbidden audits—prove the conspiracy without collapsing confidence in the Priory.
    

---

## One-liner

_Eridia is a refined, resource-rich duchy in decline—its oaks and silver still coveted, its courts elegant but cash-poor, and its future balanced between prudent restoration and a gilded surrender to Solari influence._
