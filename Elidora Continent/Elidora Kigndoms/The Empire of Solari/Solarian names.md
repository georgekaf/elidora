Solarian names reflect the grandeur, mysticism, and cultural sophistication of **The Empire of Solari**. Rooted in their connection to celestial themes, divine favor, and imperial ambition, Solarian names often carry an air of regality, elegance, and otherworldly inspiration. Their naming conventions frequently incorporate references to stars, light, fire, and the heavens. Surnames are often derived from noble houses, celestial phenomena, or esteemed professions, emphasizing the Empire's deep ties to its divine heritage and its structured aristocracy.

### **Naming Style and Roleplaying Tips**

- **First Names**: Solarian first names are grand and often carry celestial or divine undertones. Male names are powerful and commanding, while female names are ethereal and radiant, reflecting their connection to the sun, stars, and divine glory.
- **Surnames**: Solarian surnames are symbolic and poetic, often tied to celestial imagery (e.g., "Starcrest," "Dawnspire") or fire/light motifs (e.g., "Flameborn," "Ignatius"). They emphasize lineage, divine favor, and connection to Solari's celestial themes.
- **Titles and Epithets**: In Solarian society, noble and high-ranking individuals often add epithets to their names (e.g., "Aurelian Solvaris, Scion of the Sun" or "Lyra Celestine, Hand of the Empire"). These serve as markers of divine favor, achievements, or ancestral legacy.
- **Mystical Undertones**: The Solarian culture emphasizes mysticism and divine connections, so their names often evoke an aura of destiny or higher purpose. Using celestial or divine metaphors in conversations can enhance the immersion of their cultural identity.
