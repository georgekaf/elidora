**People swear loyalty to a written constitution (“the Free Charter”)—not to any person or dynasty.**

### What that means in practice

- **The oath target:** “I will uphold the Charter” (its rights, courts, and elected bodies), not “I will obey Lord X forever.”
    
- **Who swears it:** Militia, officials, judges, guilds, new citizens, even foreign volunteers who want protections.
    
- **Who receives it:** A **Charter Court** magistrate or a **Conclave** clerk, never a noble.
    

### What’s inside the Free Charter (typical clauses)

- **No hereditary rule.** Offices are elected/appointed and **recallable**.
    
- **Due process.** Open ledgers, jury trials, right to counsel.
    
- **Freedom of movement & assembly.** Refuge and safe passage for defectors.
    
- **Commons & service.** Granaries, roads, and beacons funded by **work-days** or coin.
    
- **Muster limits.** Military power cycles back to civilians after a crisis.
    

### How it differs from feudal homage

|Feudal Homage|Free Charter Oath|
|---|---|
|Personal loyalty to a lord|Institutional loyalty to the Charter|
|Lifelong unless released|Time-bound office; recallable|
|Land by vassalage|Land-use shares via the commune|
|Private justice|Public courts & published accounts|

### Enforcement & symbols

- **Breaking the oath** = Charter crime (fines, exile, loss of shares); repeated offenders can be **outlawed**.
    
- Oath-takers often wear a small **broken-link token** (chain link split)—same icon as on the flag.
    

### Quick table use

- PCs can **swear the Charter** for legal protections and access to safehouses.
    
- Refusing the oath? You can still trade, but you won’t get **Conclave protection** or jury rights if things go sideways.
  
  
  # The Free Charter of Eldoria

_(Η Ελεύθερη Χάρτα της Ελντόριας)_

## Preamble

We, the free communes of Eldoria, bind ourselves **to law not lords**, to common defense, open ledgers, and the dignity of every person. Let the falcon keep watch and the chain lie broken.

---

## Article I — First Principles

1. **Sovereignty rests in the communes** united by this Charter.
    
2. **No hereditary rule.** Office follows trust and term, not blood.
    
3. **Transparency:** public ledgers, open councils, free printing of accounts.
    

## Article II — Rights & Protections

1. **Due process:** no seizure, prison, or punishment without public charge and jury.
    
2. **Speech & assembly:** the right to meet, publish, and petition.
    
3. **Free movement & sanctuary:** refugees and defectors may claim safe passage.
    
4. **Conscience & worship:** no state creed; temples keep open books.
    
5. **Arms & self-defense:** citizens may keep arms; militias answer to civil councils.
    

## Article III — Commons & Duties

1. **Commons:** roads, beacons, levees, and granaries are held in common.
    
2. **Service:** every adult owes **work-days** or coin in lieu (posted publicly).
    
3. **Education & craft:** communes maintain schools and apprenticeships open to all.
    

## Article IV — Governance

1. **Free Conclave:** recallable delegates from each commune legislate treaties, coin, and defense.
    
2. **Marshal of the Free:** one-year, non-consecutive coordinator of militias & logistics; subject to recall by 2/3 Conclave.
    
3. **Audit Brigades:** elected accountants with full access to books and stores.
    

## Article V — Justice

1. **Charter Courts** ride circuit; verdicts by **citizen juries**.
    
2. **Outlawry** applies only after two convictions or flight from judgment.
    
3. **Restitution before pain:** fines, service, and repair preferred over bodily punishments.
    

## Article VI — Defense

1. **Militias** are raised by communes; officers chosen by merit, confirmed by councils.
    
2. **No standing army** beyond wardens and rangers except during a declared **Muster** (expires with crisis).
    
3. **War aims** must be published; secret wars are forbidden.
    

## Article VII — Coin & Provision

1. The Conclave mints **Falcons** (silver) and **Links** (copper); issues **Freedom Notes** against timber and grain reserves.
    
2. Rationing and price caps require **open vote** and **weekly publication** of stocks.
    
3. No debt may bind a person’s body; **chattel slavery is forbidden**.
    

## Article VIII — Foreigners & Treaties

1. Guests obey the Charter while within Eldoria and may claim **guest-right** at posted houses.
    
2. Treaties require a **dual majority**: Conclave vote + commune referendum where applicable.
    

## Article IX — Amendments

1. Amend by **two Conclave sessions** and **majority of communes**.
    
2. Articles II (Rights) and VII.3 (No slavery) are **entrenched**; may not be repealed.
    

## Article X — Emergencies

1. Invasion, famine, or plague allows **Crisis Powers** for 90 days: fast levies, rationing, requisition with receipts.
    
2. All crisis decrees **expire** unless renewed by open vote; all records published after.
    

---

## Oath of the Free (ceremonial)

> _“I swear by falcon and broken chain to uphold the Free Charter, to answer summons of council and jury, to keep the commons in my care, and to yield my office at term. I bind myself to law, not to any lord.”_

**Seal:** A silver falcon volant grasping a broken chain within a ring of laurel.  
**Motto:** _Unbound, Unbowed._ _(Χωρίς Δεσμά, Αλύγιστοι)_
