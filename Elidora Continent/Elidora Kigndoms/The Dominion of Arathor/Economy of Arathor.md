Here’s a compact, table-ready snapshot of **the Dominion of Arathor** right now—useful for play, planning, or a wiki drop-in.

# Overview

- **Type:** Militarized **dominion** of clans and noble houses under a **High Duke** (hereditary) + a **War Council** (clan champions, guild factors, temple envoy).
    
- **Position:** Land bridge between **Solari** and **Drakonia**, sea-facing toward **Verosia**. Sits on caravan roads, causeway forts, and choke-points—small realm, outsized leverage.
    
- **Current Mood:** Proud, suspicious, slightly hungry. Public face = order; back rooms = factional knives. Conspiracy (3 years old) quietly realigns contracts and garrisons.
    

---

# Economy (what keeps the lights on)

|Sector|What Arathor Produces|Who Controls It|Frictions|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Iron & Tool Forges**|Weapons, tools, wagon fittings|Forge Guild + sword-holding houses|Price spikes from Drakonia/Verosia demand|
|**Border Logistics**|Caravan warehousing, road patrols, ferries|Roadwardens (crown) + Merchant Syndics|Tolls vs. “escort fees” (shakedowns)|
|**Grain & Herding**|Barley, oats, hardy cattle/sheep|Clan estates + temple granaries|Weather risk; levy seizures in crisis|
|**Timber & Charcoal**|From Imp Copse & ridgewoods|Woodsmen’s Brotherhood|Poaching by Solari contractors|
|**Salt & Fish**|Coastal hamlets (Seadrift, Vaelstead)|Harbor reeves|Smuggling to dodge war taxes|
|**Contract Steel**|Custom gear for foreign clients|Mixed guilds|Export limits when Muster is called|

**Money & Trade:** Silver **stag-marks** (mint at Clankford); tariffs light on staples, heavier on arms. Credit through **house letters** (noble-backed notes). Bribery = “expedited levy” in polite speech.

# Society (who answers to whom)

|Layer|Power Holder|Texture & Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Crown**|High Duke at **Aratharion**|Commands forts, roads, mint; “Muster Law” can conscript carts/stock|
|**War Council**|Duke + clan champions + guildmaster + temple prior|Balances clan honor vs. coin; votes simple majority, Duke can break ties|
|**Clans/Houses**|Bannered families with sworn levies|Own most land; feud law curbed but alive|
|**Guilds**|Forge, Caravan, River, Charcoal|Urban muscle; fund road repairs (for a cut)|
|**Temple of the Banner Sun**|State cult of discipline & vows|Blesses levies; runs poor granaries|
|**Commonfolk**|Smallholders, drovers, ferrymen|Owe work-days on roads/levees; protected by guest-right|

**Law & Order:** Feuds legal only after **Stonecourt** verdict; outlawry for striking caravans under Arathori seal. Torture illegal; **oath-ordeals** still used in rural courts.

# Politics (who’s pulling at the threads)

**Public blocs**

- **Stalwarts:** Keep neutrality; sell logistics to all sides. (Many small houses, road guilds, temple moderates.)
    
- **Iron Standard:** Quietly **pro-Solari**; want a protectorate deal and big forge contracts.
    
- **Tide Accord:** **Pro-Drakonia**; push naval cooperation & pilotage guarantees.
    
- **Sea Handshake:** **Pro-Verosia**; favor scholarly exchange, arcanite tooling, and soft alliance.
    

**The Conspiracy (3 years running)**

- **Duke Gideon** (pragmatist hardliner), **Baroness Celeste** (coin & contracts), **Sir Reginald** (levy commander).
    
- Tactics: manipulated tolls, “training deployments” near Drakonian beacons, and exclusive ore-forwarding deals—meant to starve Drakonia of coin and force Verosia into dependence while keeping Solari happy in public and indebted in private.
    

---

# Security Posture

- **Forts & Lines:** Stone keeps at **Thalvessan** and **Skarnthor** anchor the inland road; watchtowers on the north causeways.
    
- **Forces:** 3,500–5,000 levies under arms in a season; 600 roadwardens; 300 river marines; 200 elite **Banner Knights** (heavy).
    
- **Doctrine:** Hold roads, deny fords, bleed raiders. Prefer fines and seizures to pitched battles.
    
- **Threat Board:** Drakonian privateers, Solari “advisers” in border villages, Eldorian smugglers, inland bandit companies with noble cover.
    

---

# Culture & Faith

- **Virtues:** Oath, drill, austere hospitality. Ceremonial duels at dawn; winners buy the loser’s breakfast.
    
- **Holy Days:** **Bannerday** (musters), **Stonecourt** (law), **Ancestor’s Rest** (feasts)—from the Arathori 10-day week.
    
- **Zodiac lean:** Many officers boast **Verrinor (Blade)**, **Highcrown/Light** equivalents; seers mark **Seerthread** days for campaigns.

# Current Flashpoints & Hooks

1. **Helmfirth Seizures:** Roadwardens (under Reginald) impound Drakonian cargo “for inspection.” Retaliation imminent.
    
2. **Clankford Mint Leak:** Celeste’s factor sells debased stag-marks; guilds demand heads.
    
3. **Banner Knights Missing:** A patrol vanishes near **Imp Copse**; tracks show foreign gear.
    
4. **Temple Split:** Younger priests critique the conspiracy; a sermon almost names names.
    
5. **Verosian Offer:** A research grant to upgrade causeways—strings: arcanite quotas and a scholar with too many questions.
    

---

# Quick GM Switches

- **Muster Law On/Off:** Flip to raise stakes; activates levies, toll hikes, and commandeered wagons.
    
- **Court Day:** Run a **Stonecourt** session to settle feuds or expose bribery.
    
- **Coin Check:** Roll for stag-mark trust; if it slips, prices surge and tempers flare.
    

**One-liner:**  
_Arathor is a disciplined hinge-state—small in land, huge in leverage—where clan honor, guild coin, and a three-year conspiracy grind against each other under the watch of the Banner Sun._
