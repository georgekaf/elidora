Arathorian names reflect the proud, militaristic, and disciplined nature of **The Dominion of Arathor**.  

Influenced by the Dominion's structured society and martial heritage, these names carry an air of authority, dignity, and strength. They often have a regal or classical tone, combining elements of Latin, Germanic, and Old English traditions. Surnames frequently denote military ranks, professions, or ancestral achievements, emphasizing their devotion to hierarchy and honor.

### **Naming Style and Roleplaying Tips**

- **First Names**: Arathorian names are formal and commanding, often with a Romanesque or noble sound. Male names tend to be bold and decisive, while female names are elegant yet strong, reflecting the Dominion’s respect for discipline and leadership.
- **Surnames**: Frequently tied to military or ancestral accomplishments, they highlight an individual's place within the Dominion's hierarchical society. For instance, "Ironshield" could signify a family of warriors or defenders, while "Vaelmont" might represent nobility or rulership over a mountainous region.
- **Titles and Ranks**: Arathorians often introduce themselves with their titles (e.g., "General Marcus Tyralon" or "Dame Selene Vaelora"). Status and accomplishments are critical in their culture, so these are often used to establish credibility or authority in conversation.
