The Dominion of Arathor

A small but influential country that is caught in the middle of the conspiracy. 
It resides between Draconia Solari and borders Verosia by sea only.

Short version: in Elidora terms, **a dominion is a semi-sovereign realm ruled by a ducal house under a binding charter**, not a fully independent kingdom. Arathor fits because of these traits:

- **Charter > Crown:** Arathor is governed by a **ducal charter** (the Dominion Charter of Aratharion), not a royal writ. The High Duke holds **dominion rights**—broad internal authority—but **no right to claim a crown** or style as “king.”
    
- **Overlordship is legal, not daily:** It acknowledges historical **overlord claims** (old imperial patents and sea-rights), but pays them as **treaty obligations**—transit guarantees, fixed toll caps, muster support—rather than feudal submission.
    
- **War powers are limited by charter:** The High Duke can declare a **Muster** inside Arathor, but **cannot wage foreign war or annex lands** without War Council assent and notice to signatory neighbors.
    
- **Sovereignty split:**
    
    - **Internal:** full control—courts, taxes, levies, mint (**stag-marks**), roads/forts.
        
    - **External:** constrained—treaty tariffs, pilotage neutrality, and limits on permanent alliances or foreign garrisons.
        
- **Title & council structure:** Dominion rulers are **High Dukes** advised by a **War Council** (clan champions + guild + temple). That council is **charter-mandated**, which is why it isn’t a simple duchy or march.
    
- **Neutrality obligations:** As a dominion on a strategic corridor, Arathor must **keep roads open and harbors neutral** for chartered trade—one reason both Solari and Drakonia tolerate it.
    
- **Religious status:** The **Temple of the Banner Sun** is a **state cult under charter** (not a royal church). It legitimizes musters and audits granaries—another dominion hallmark.
    
- **Coinage but not standards:** Arathor mints **silver stag-marks**, yet accepts **regional gold standards** set by larger powers; kingdoms set the standard, dominions follow it.
    

**One-liner:**  
_Arathor is a charter-bound realm with kinglike powers at home and treaty-bound limits abroad—strong enough to rule itself, not free enough to call itself a kingdom._
