# How Confederation of Dragonborns formed

## 1) Before the Confederation — the Nine Broods

For centuries the jungles and marsh-archipelagos around the **Sea of Medeia** were home to scattered **dragonborn broods**:

- **Sea Clans** (islets, mangrove towns): pilots, pearl-divers, tide-raiders.
    
- **Marsh Clans** (reed cities, causeways): salt-farmers, herbalists, swamp wardens.
    
- **Ridge Clans** (basalt hills): iron/obsidian workers, oath-duel champions.  
    They shared **guest-right**, duel-law, and draconic honor codes—but answered to their own matriarchs/patriarchs.
    

## 2) The Catalysts

- **The Wound & the Verosia–Solari crisis** choked sea lanes; outside powers leaned hard on Medeia’s routes.
    
- **Arcanite** (from Verosian trade) created a black market; pirates and Solari “advisers” pushed for bases along the littoral.
    
- **Slave-raids & press-gangs** hit small broods; a few Ridge war-bands struck back, escalating reprisals.
    

## 3) The First Muster at **Thalric’s Landing**

Envoys from nine great broods met on neutral ground after a Solari flotilla torched three marsh villages “by mistake.”

- They swore the **Oath of Common Scale**: _“No brood stands alone before foreign fire.”_
    
- They elected **two war-speakers** (one Sea, one Ridge) to coordinate defense for one season only—temporary power to calm the proud.
    

## 4) The Charter of the **Seven Scales**

The “temporary” pact worked—raids dropped, sea control returned—so the broods hammered it into law:

- **The Seven Scales (Wyrmcouncil):** Sea, Marsh, Ridge, Forge, Lore, Trade, and Faith. Each sends one elder (rotating every 3 years).
    
- **The Ash-Seat (Warchair):** a single commander chosen only during declared Muster; steps down when threats end.
    
- **Law of Braids:** local customs remain, but **oaths, guest-right, and inter-brood trials** are confederate jurisdiction.
    
- **Mutual Waters:** harbors and dredged channels kept neutral; light tolls fund beacons and marsh causeways.
    

## 5) Institutions that made it stick

- **Hearth-Marks:** shared waystations on stilt pilings; any dragonborn can claim shelter for a night—sacred and warded.
    
- **Loreclaw Archive:** swamp-stone vault that records **oaths, boundaries, and feud-settlements**; keeps copies of external treaties.
    
- **Forge-Circles:** mixed-brood smiths standardize fittings, river-armor, and **breath-tempered** blades (lightning-quenched, frost-hardened).
    
- **Tide Courts:** traveling magistrates (often Marsh elders) settle disputes between broods before they fester.
    

## 6) Military & strategy (why it scares neighbors)

- **Skirmish navy:** shallow-draft cutters and paddle-rams that vanish into reeds; night beacons coordinated by storm-seers.
    
- **Ward-bands:** small elite units (10–30) tailored by ancestry—fire shock, frost anchors, lightning relays.
    
- **Causeway forts:** low, brutal, and hard to siege; they flood the field rather than meet heavy infantry head-on.
    

## 7) Economy & leverage

- **Marsh staples:** salt, fish, reeds, rare resins, alchemical herbs.
    
- **Forge-exports:** breath-tempered gear and rivercraft hulls.
    
- **Pilotage monopoly:** Confederation pilots guide all Medeia traffic; violators “lose their way” among the shoals.
    

## 8) Religion & signs

- Public rites follow **Vaelun (will/strife)** and **Seren (fate/omens)**; **Lunareth** guides funerals and guest-right.
    
- Many leaders born under **Verrinor (Blade)**, **Zephyren (Whirlwind)**, or **Kaelir (Rift)**—the Confederation reads this as a mandate to act fast and change hard.
    

## 9) Current tensions (hooks)

- **Sea vs Ridge:** Sea Clans want freebooting letters of marque; Ridge Clans demand stricter honor courts.
    
- **Solari pressure:** offers “mutual defense” pacts that smell like protectorates.
    
- **Verosian diplomacy:** Verosia pays for pilotage and smithing—some broods want a formal alliance, others fear entanglement.
    
- **Smoldering feud:** two broods argue over a **sunken Arcanite cache**; Wyrmcouncil races to arbitrate before blades come out.
