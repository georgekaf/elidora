### **Naming Style and Roleplaying Tips**

- **First Names**: Orlais first names often have a romantic and lyrical quality, reflecting their culture's love for beauty and refinement. Male names project a sense of nobility and confidence, while female names are graceful and elegant.
- **Surnames**: Orlais surnames are often tied to nature (e.g., "Fontaine," "Beaumont"), status (e.g., "Chevalier," "Chastain"), or beauty (e.g., "Lafleur," "Beaulieu"). Noble surnames may carry a "de" or "d'" prefix, indicating a connection to a specific place or estate (e.g., "d'Argent," "de Valmont").
- **Titles and Formality**: Orlais names are often accompanied by titles, especially for nobility (e.g., "Marquis Étienne Montclair" or "Duchess Juliette Blanchet"). Addressing others formally is deeply ingrained in their culture and should reflect their status.
- **Cultural Flair**: When roleplaying an Orlais character, consider using phrases, mannerisms, and tones that reflect their emphasis on elegance and high culture. Their speech may include poetic metaphors or subtle hints of superiority.
- **A Sense of Drama**: Orlais society might value dramatic expressions of art, love, or loyalty, which can be reflected in how their characters introduce themselves or narrate their achievements.
