# Elara Gearwind — Female Half-Elf Artificer

## Character Concept

**Name:** Elara Gearwind  
**Race:** Half-Elf  
**Class:** Artificer  
**Role:** Inventor, arcane engineer, magical problem-solver  
**Campaign Location:** Stormwreck Isle

Elara Gearwind is a female half-elf artificer who blends magical theory with practical invention. She channels spells through tools, devices, alchemical components, lenses, gauntlets, and other crafted objects rather than casting in the manner of a traditional wizard or sorcerer.

Her style emphasizes ingenuity, preparation, and the ability to turn ordinary objects into magical solutions.

## Visual Design

Elara should be depicted as a **full-body female half-elf artificer**.

### Drawing Style

The drawing style should have:

- A vibrant and dynamic feel.
- A blend of **steampunk** and **fantasy** aesthetics.
- A **semi-realistic comic book** look.
- Sharp, defined line work.
- Strong silhouettes and readable gear details.
- Earthy tones such as brown, bronze, leather, brass, and dark red.
- Bright magical accents, such as blue, green, or violet arcane glow.
- Clothing and equipment that emphasize her role as an artificer.

### Appearance

Elara has:

- Half-elven features.
- Slightly pointed ears.
- Graceful but practical facial features.
- Hair tied back for workshop or adventuring use.
- A confident, alert posture.

### Outfit and Equipment

She wears:

- A long leather coat or reinforced artificer jacket.
- Light armor mixed with metal plates.
- A utility belt filled with tools, vials, gears, pouches, and small inventions.
- Enchanted goggles, either worn over her eyes or pushed up onto her forehead.
- Gloves or a runic gauntlet.
- Boots suitable for rough island terrain.
- Arcane-mechanical devices attached to her gear.

Possible held items:

- A glowing magical device.
- A mechanical projector.
- A runic gauntlet.
- A small alchemical grenade.
- A compact arcane lens.
- A tinker's tool or multi-tool.

## Artificer Class Summary

Artificers are masters of invention who use ingenuity and magic to unlock extraordinary capabilities in objects. They treat magic as a complex system that can be decoded, engineered, and harnessed through spells, tools, and inventions.

They use a variety of tools to channel arcane power. An artificer might:

- Use alchemist's supplies to create a potent elixir.
- Use calligrapher's supplies to inscribe a sigil of power.
- Use tinker's tools to craft a temporary charm.
- Use thieves' tools or artisan's tools as spellcasting focuses.

Their magic is tied to their tools and talents, making them uniquely suited to creating the right tool for the job.

---

## Magical Tinkering

At 1st level, an artificer can invest a spark of magic into mundane objects.

To use this ability, the artificer must have thieves' tools or artisan's tools in hand. They touch a Tiny nonmagical object as an action and give it one magical property.

Possible properties:

1. The object sheds bright light in a 5-foot radius and dim light for an additional 5 feet.
2. Whenever tapped by a creature, the object emits a recorded message heard up to 10 feet away. The message is spoken when the property is bestowed and can be no more than 6 seconds long.
3. The object continuously emits an odor or nonverbal sound, such as wind, waves, or chirping, perceivable up to 10 feet away.
4. A static visual effect appears on one surface of the object. This can be a picture, up to 25 words of text, lines, shapes, or a mixture of these elements.

The property lasts indefinitely. The artificer can end it early by touching the object as an action.

The artificer can affect multiple objects, but only up to a number equal to their Intelligence modifier, with a minimum of one. If they exceed this maximum, the oldest property ends.

---

## Spellcasting

Artificers study the workings of magic and cast spells by channeling magic through objects. To observers, they do not appear to cast spells conventionally; instead, they produce wonders through mundane items and unusual inventions.

### Tools Required

An artificer produces spell effects through tools. They must have a spellcasting focus in hand when casting artificer spells.

Valid focuses include:

- Thieves' tools.
- Artisan's tools.
- Any item bearing one of their infusions after gaining Infuse Item at 2nd level.

The artificer must be proficient with the tool to use it this way.

### Cantrips

At 1st level, an artificer knows two cantrips from the artificer spell list. They learn more at higher levels. When they gain a level in the class, they can replace one artificer cantrip with another.

### Preparing and Casting Spells

Artificers prepare spells from the artificer spell list. The number of prepared spells equals:

**Intelligence modifier + half artificer level, rounded down**

Minimum: one spell.

Prepared spells must be of a level for which the artificer has spell slots.

Artificers regain all expended spell slots after a long rest. They can change prepared spells after a long rest by spending time tinkering with their spellcasting focuses: at least 1 minute per spell level for each spell on the list.

### Spellcasting Ability

Artificers use **Intelligence** as their spellcasting ability.

**Spell save DC:**  
8 + proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier

**Spell attack modifier:**  
proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier

### Ritual Casting

An artificer can cast an artificer spell as a ritual if:

- The spell has the ritual tag.
- The artificer has the spell prepared.

---

## Infuse Item

At 2nd level, artificers can imbue mundane objects with magical infusions, turning them into magic items.

### Infusions Known

When gaining this feature, the artificer learns four artificer infusions. More are learned at later levels. Whenever the artificer gains a level, they can replace one known infusion with another.

### Infusing an Item

Whenever the artificer finishes a long rest, they can touch a nonmagical object and imbue it with one of their infusions.

Rules:

- An infusion works only on certain kinds of objects.
- If the item requires attunement, the artificer can attune to it immediately.
- Each infusion can exist in only one object at a time.
- One object can bear only one infusion at a time.
- If the artificer exceeds their maximum number of infused items, the oldest infusion ends.
- If an infusion ends on a container such as a bag of holding, its contents harmlessly appear in and around its space.

An infusion remains indefinitely, but if the artificer dies, it vanishes after a number of days equal to their Intelligence modifier, minimum 1 day.

## The Right Tool for the Job

At 3rd level, the artificer can magically create exactly the tool they need.

With thieves' tools or artisan's tools in hand, they can create one set of artisan's tools in an unoccupied space within 5 feet.

This requires 1 hour of uninterrupted work, which can coincide with a short or long rest.

The tools are nonmagical and vanish when the artificer uses this feature again.

---

## Ability Score Improvement

At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, the artificer can increase ability scores:

- Increase one ability score by 2.
- Or increase two ability scores by 1.

No ability score can be increased above 20 using this feature.

---

## Tool Expertise

At 6th level, the artificer's proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check that uses their proficiency with a tool.

---

## Flash of Genius

At 7th level, the artificer can solve problems under pressure.

When the artificer or another creature they can see within 30 feet makes an ability check or saving throw, the artificer can use their reaction to add their Intelligence modifier to the roll.

Uses per long rest:

**Intelligence modifier**, minimum once.

---

## Magic Item Adept

At 10th level:

- The artificer can attune to up to four magic items at once.
- When crafting a common or uncommon magic item, it takes one quarter of the normal time and costs half the usual gold.

---

## Spell-Storing Item

At 11th level, the artificer can store a spell in an object.

After a long rest, they can touch:

- One simple weapon.
- One martial weapon.
- Or one item usable as a spellcasting focus.

They store a 1st-level or 2nd-level artificer spell in it.

Requirements:

- The spell must require 1 action to cast.
- The artificer does not need to have the spell prepared.

While holding the object, a creature can use an action to produce the spell's effect using the artificer's spellcasting ability modifier.

If the spell requires concentration, the creature using the item must concentrate.

The spell remains until:

- It has been used a number of times equal to twice the artificer's Intelligence modifier, minimum twice.
- Or the artificer uses this feature again.

---

## Magic Item Savant

At 14th level:

- The artificer can attune to up to five magic items at once.
- The artificer ignores all class, race, spell, and level requirements when attuning to or using a magic item.

---

## Magic Item Master

At 18th level, the artificer can attune to up to six magic items at once.

---

## Soul of Artifice

At 20th level, the artificer develops a mystical connection to their magic items.

Benefits:

- The artificer gains a +1 bonus to all saving throws for each magic item they are attuned to.
- If reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, they can use their reaction to end one artificer infusion and drop to 1 hit point instead.

# Stormwreck Isle

## Overview

Stormwreck Isle is a mountainous island in the Sea of Swords. It lies about 3.5 miles from the coast of Faerûn near the High Road, south of Neverwinter. The island is about 5 miles wide.

Stormwreck Isle is a beacon to dragons and is suffused with the magic of countless dragons who died there. This left a spiritual wound across the island, causing unpredictable magical phenomena.

---

## Geography

Stormwreck Isle is rocky and dangerous.

Important features include:

- Basalt cliffs.
- Sea caves.
- Hot springs.
- Sharp coastal rocks.
- Treacherous shoals.
- Massive gold dragon bones along the northwest shoal.
- Seagrow Caves on the southwest side of the island.
- Volcanic vents connected to the tomb of the red dragon Sharruth.

The toxic fumes in Seagrow Caves are said to originate from Sharruth's volcanic tomb.

---

## Flora and Fauna

Flora:

- White wildflowers grow on the northeast side of the island.
- Mushrooms grow in the caves.
- Violet fungus can sometimes be found underground.

Fauna and creatures:

- Seagulls along the shores.
- Giant octopuses in the surrounding waters.
- Stirges in the caves.
- Fume drakes near hot springs and volcanic activity.
- Salamanders or salamander young may appear underground where the barrier to the Elemental Plane of Fire is weakened.
- Kobolds.
- Myconids.
- Harpies.
- Zombies along the northern coast.

---

## History

Stormwreck Isle was formed in ancient times by volcanic activity caused when the mighty red dragon **Sharruth** was entombed on the sea floor by three metallic dragons:

- **Astalagan**, a bronze dragon.
- **Clyssavar**, a gold dragon.
- **Turadaer**, a brass dragon.

Since then, the island has drawn dragons to it. Metallic and chromatic dragons fought many battles there, and the magic of slain dragons seeped into the land.

A group of scholars later built the **Clifftop Observatory** to study and harness the island's ambient magic.

In the latter half of the 14th century DR, the blue dragon **Eldenemir** tried to use the Observatory to seize control of the island's magic. He was slain by the bronze dragon **Runara**.

About a century later, Eldenemir's grandchild, the blue wyrmling **Sparkrender**, came to the island. He occupied the ruined Observatory and planned to awaken the spirits of the dead dragons, using their power to become a god. He imprisoned the bronze wyrmling **Aidron** and intended to sacrifice him to empower the ritual.

Another tragedy occurred when the ship **Compass Rose** wrecked on the northern rocks. A dying passenger named **Aletha** prayed to Orcus to be reunited with her husband Brastos, creating a curse that caused those who died in shipwrecks around the island to rise as undead.

Rumors persist that Sharruth may still exist in some form deep below the island.

---

### Clifftop Observatory

### Dragon's Rest

A monastery dedicated to Bahamut, known as a place of wisdom and peace.

### Seagrow Cave

A basalt cave located above the tomb of Sharruth.

# Elara Gearwind's Backstory for Stormwreck Isle

Elara Gearwind came to Stormwreck Isle chasing the kind of mystery only an artificer could love: a land where magic had soaked into stone, bone, steam, and ruin.

Born between two worlds as a half-elf, Elara never felt fully at home among either humans or elves. She found belonging instead in tools, formulas, gears, lenses, and half-finished inventions. Her talent was not simply casting magic, but understanding how magic moved through matter.

When she heard of Stormwreck Isle, a place scarred by ancient dragon deaths and unstable magical phenomena, she became obsessed. To most travelers, the island was a cursed rock of shipwrecks, undead, dragon bones, toxic fumes, and violent storms. To Elara, it was a living laboratory.

She traveled to Dragon's Rest under the pretense of repairing monastery tools and studying the island's strange energies. In truth, she hoped to understand how dragon magic had become embedded in the island itself. She began crafting devices that could detect magical surges, measure elemental heat, and capture faint echoes of draconic power.

Elara is not reckless, but curiosity often pulls her toward danger. The ruins of the Clifftop Observatory, the fumes of Seagrow Caves, and the dragon bones along the shoals all call to her. She believes the island's magic can be understood and perhaps even stabilized.

Now, with Sparkrender's threat rising and the old wounds of Stormwreck Isle stirring again, Elara must decide whether she is merely a researcher or someone willing to stand between the island and another cycle of dragon-fueled destruction.

# Faerie Fire

## Spell Description

**Faerie Fire**  
**Level:** 1  
**School:** Evocation  
**Casting Time:** Action  
**Range:** 60 feet  
**Components:** V  
**Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 minute

Each object in a 20-foot cube within range is outlined in blue, green, or violet light, chosen by the caster.

Any creature in the area when the spell is cast is also outlined in light if it fails a Dexterity saving throw.

For the duration, objects and affected creatures shed dim light in a 10-foot radius.

Any attack roll against an affected creature or object has advantage if the attacker can see it, and the affected creature or object cannot benefit from being invisible.

---

# How Elara Could Cast Faerie Fire

As an artificer, Elara does not need to cast Faerie Fire like a traditional spellcaster. She can express the verbal component as a command phrase, activation word, formula, or technical incantation while using tools or devices to produce the effect.

## Mechanical Projector

Elara uses a wrist-mounted projector or small brass-and-glass device. She turns a dial to choose blue, green, or violet light, then speaks the activation phrase.

Example incantation:

**"Illuminate and reveal."**

The device releases a burst of arcane particles that spread through the target area and cling to creatures and objects as glowing outlines.

## Alchemical Flare Grenade

Elara throws a small alchemical capsule filled with luminous reagent and arcane dust. When it bursts, it releases glowing mist that outlines everything in the area.

Example incantation:

**"By light's grasp, be seen."**

The mist clings to hidden shapes, invisible creatures, armor edges, weapons, and objects.

## Runic Gauntlet

Elara wears a gauntlet etched with small runes and fitted with lenses or crystal nodes. She presses a rune with her thumb and speaks the spell phrase.

Example incantation:

**"Reveal the unseen."**

The gauntlet flashes and projects a pulse of magical light into the chosen area.

## Magical Lens or Goggles

Elara adjusts enchanted goggles or a handheld lens, aligning gears and glass plates to focus the spell.

Example incantation:

**"Activate photon dispersion matrix."**

A beam of light passes through the lens, scatters into the area, and outlines creatures and objects in magical color.

## Clockwork Firefly Swarm

Elara releases several tiny clockwork fireflies from a vial or shoulder-mounted dispenser. They scatter into the area, leaving trails of colored light.

Example incantation:

**"Pattern lock. Mark all movement."**

The fireflies burst into sparks that outline targets in the spell's glow.

## Artificer Flavor Summary

For Elara, Faerie Fire is not simply a burst of fairy-like light. It is a calibrated reveal-field produced by tools, reagents, runes, and arcane engineering. The spell's magic remains the same, but its presentation reflects her identity as an inventor.
