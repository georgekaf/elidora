## Core Setting

**Elidora** is a diverse and politically charged continent, filled with kingdoms, empires, federations, and hidden orders. Its history is shaped by gods, cosmic truths, Nexus Souls, wars, conspiracies, and the lingering consequences of forbidden knowledge.

The current age is defined by diplomacy, betrayal, magical secrets, and the escalating conflict between powerful states, especially **Verosia** and **Solari**.

## Nations of Elidora

### Kingdom of Verosia

A prosperous and influential kingdom ruled by **King Alistair of House Ravenshield**. Verosia is guided by the **Council of the Crown**, a powerful advisory body representing magic, faith, war, diplomacy, nature, craftsmanship, and hidden knowledge.

**Insignia:** A golden griffon rampant on a deep blue shield, with a crown of laurel leaves encircling its head. The griffon’s talons clutch a radiant sun, symbolizing strength, wisdom, and leadership.

**Political Structure:** Monarchy with a ruling council.

**Strengths:** Alliances, intelligence networks, disciplined armies, knightly orders, arcane advisors, magical research.

**Current Themes:**

- Internal dissent and rebel pressure.
- External pressure from Solari.
- Shifting alliances with Calandria, Drakonia, Valoria, and other powers.
- The Council may be compromised, especially by **Archmage Arcturus**, who secretly works for Solari.

#### Verosian Economy

Verosia has a diversified, semi-feudal economy with strong regional specialties.

**Major sectors:**

- Agriculture and livestock in central and southern mainland settlements.
- Forestry and hunting in forest regions such as Northhollow, Duskwatch, and Gloomwood.
- Fishing and maritime trade through island and coastal cities such as Highspire, Marrowdeep, Saltmere, and Harrspire.
- Mining and metalwork near eastern mountain settlements and Ironmarch Keep.
- Trade and craftsmanship in Caer Virelion, Frostmere, and other major cities.
- Magical goods, alchemy, books, pottery, and guild-produced craft goods.

#### Arcanite / Veinstone

A magical ore found in Verosia, especially near the Iron Mountains and settlements such as Ironmarch Keep and Stonebrook.

**Known names:** Arcanite or Veinstone.

**Properties:**

- Enhances magical conductivity.
- Used in enchantments, wands, spell foci, magical constructs, arcane engines, and magical weapons/armor.
- Emits ambient arcana and can be dangerous to handle unprotected.

**Political significance:**

- Verosia likely claims a crown monopoly over Arcanite.
- Licensed guilds and nobles may legally mine or refine it.
- Smugglers, black markets, Solari agents, and rival states seek access to it.

**Guild of Deep Flame:** A mixed dwarven, human, and gnomish guild in Stonebrook specializing in magical metallurgy.

### Empire of Solari

A proud, autocratic, expansionist empire ruled by an emperor. It has a vast military, a powerful state bureaucracy, disciplined legions, and a propaganda-driven hatred of Verosia.

**Insignia:** A blazing sun with twelve radiant beams on a crimson field. At the center of the sun, a golden phoenix rises with outstretched wings, surrounded by flames, representing rebirth, power, and unyielding ambition.

**Conspirators:**

- Archmage Arcturus
- Lord Alaric
- Lady Sabrina

**Date of conspiracy:** Began 5 years ago.

#### Solari Economy

Solari uses an imperial command economy over a capitalist spine. Private guilds and merchant houses exist, but the Sun Throne can requisition production, fix prices, redirect trade, and prioritize military needs.

**Core sectors:**

- Grain and staple works.
- Iron and foundries.
- Imperial Arcanum and state magic.
- Roads, fords, forts, and military engineering.
- Textiles and leather for uniforms, tents, and harnesses.
- Maritime and river trade.
- Arcanite imports and controlled licensing.

**Currency:** Solaris gold, Lumen silver, Sparks copper.

**Political character:**

- Autocratic emperor on the Sun Throne.
- Major ministries: War, Treasury, Imperial Arcanum.
- Censors and prefects govern provinces.
- Black Sun Office acts as secret police.

**Shadow structure:**

- Arcturus links the Arcanum to foreign plots.
- Lord Alaric controls logistics and profits from war surcharges.
- Lady Sabrina shapes propaganda and censorship.

**One-liner:** Solari is a war-shaped autocracy that runs on quotas, roads, spectacle, propaganda, and imperial ambition.

### Confederation of Drakonia

A powerful nation of dragonborn tribes living in the jungles, marshes, islands, and waterways around the **Sea of Medeia**, especially west, south, and southeast of the sea.

**Insignia:** A red dragon rampant, breathing fire upward on a black-and-gold checkered field. The dragon is surrounded by jagged mountain peaks, representing fiery will, independence, and untamed nature.

#### Formation

The Confederation formed when scattered dragonborn broods around the Sea of Medeia united after raids, foreign meddling, press-gangs, and Solari pressure threatened their survival.

They met at **Thalric’s Landing** and swore the **Oath of Common Scale**:

> No brood stands alone before foreign fire.

They later created the **Charter of the Seven Scales**.

#### Political Structure

- Confederation of broods/clans.
- Supreme body: **Wyrmcouncil of the Seven Scales**.
- Seven Scales: Sea, Marsh, Ridge, Forge, Lore, Trade, Faith.
- Temporary wartime executive: **Ash-Seat / Warchair**, elected only during a declared Muster.
- Judiciary: **Tide Courts** and **Loreclaw Archive**.

#### Economy

Drakonia’s economy is water-first and clan-driven.

**Key sectors:**

- Pilotage and navigation.
- Marsh and river shipping.
- Breath-tempered metallurgy.
- Fisheries and salterns.
- Reeds, resin, spices, alchemical herbs.
- Guard and guide contracts.
- Boatbuilding and shallow-draft craft.
- Pearls and ambergris.

**Institutions:**

- Wyrmcouncil sets tariffs and pilotage law.
- Tide Courts enforce oaths and salvage claims.
- Forge-Circles regulate breath-tempered goods.
- Hoardmark Office manages treasury.
- Loreclaw Archive records oaths, treaties, boundaries, and charts.

**Currency:** Mixed foreign coin, scale-marks, reef tokens, oath-script / scale chits.

#### Dragonborn Visual Guide

Dragonborn in Elidora should mirror D&D-style dragonborn:

- Tall, powerfully muscled, broad-shouldered.
- Draconic heads with ridges, horns, frills, or spines.
- Digitigrade legs, heavy taloned feet.
- Strong claws and scaled bodies.
- Scale colors connected to ancestry: red, brass, copper, blue, bronze, black, green, white, silver, etc.
- Armor: lamellar, scale, brigandine, reinforced shoulders, split skirts, clan sashes.
- Elite warriors may wear runed plate with ancestry motifs.

### Dominion of Arathor

A small but influential country caught in the middle of the conspiracy. It lies between Drakonia and Solari and borders Verosia only by sea.

**Insignia:** A black sword crossed with a golden hammer on a deep green field. Behind the weapons, a coiled silver dragon encircles them, symbolizing strength, resilience, and unity through might.

**Conspirators:**

- Duke Gideon of Arathor
- Baroness Celeste of Arathor
- Sir Reginald of Arathor

**Date of conspiracy:** Began 3 years ago.

#### Why it is a Dominion

Arathor is a dominion because it is a semi-sovereign, charter-bound realm ruled by a ducal house rather than a fully independent kingdom.

**Traits of the Dominion:**

- The High Duke has kinglike internal powers but cannot claim a crown.
- Charter law restricts foreign war, alliances, and permanent foreign garrisons.
- It holds internal authority over courts, taxes, levies, roads, forts, and minting.
- External sovereignty is limited by treaty obligations.
- It is governed by a High Duke and War Council.
- The Temple of the Banner Sun legitimizes musters and audits granaries.

#### Political-Economic Structure

- High Duke at Aratharion.
- War Council of clan champions, guild factors, and temple envoys.
- Strong road, caravan, forge, and border economy.
- Maintains forts and causeways.
- Small realm with outsized leverage due to strategic position.

**Economy sectors:**

- Iron and tool forges.
- Border logistics and road patrols.
- Grain and herding.
- Timber and charcoal.
- Salt and fish.
- Contract steel and arms export.

### Federation of Orlais

A collection of smaller nations that banded together for protection. It borders the Principality of Valoria to the west. Orlais is elegant, diplomatic, refined, and culturally sophisticated, with strong French and Martinique-inspired characteristics.

**Insignia:** A white dove with outstretched wings clutching a golden olive branch on a sky-blue field. Beneath the dove, a pair of clasped hands symbolizes unity, diplomacy, and dedication to harmony. Later visual versions used armored metallic gauntlets in place of bare hands.

#### Government

- Federal league of principalities, cities, and smaller states.
- Bicameral Conclave:
  - Council of Banners: rulers or envoys of member states.
  - Council of Guilds: merchants, shipwrights, bankers, artisans.
- First Arbiter: rotating presiding magistrate.
- Federal competencies: treaties, customs union, navy, roads, port standards, coinage, mutual defense.

#### Economy

**Major sectors:**

- Maritime trade and finance.
- Shipbuilding.
- Textiles and fashion.
- Glass, optics, jewelry, instruments.
- Wine and olive oil.
- Printing and theater.

**Currency:** Écu, Or, Sou.

#### Culture

- Sky-blue banners, white doves, clasped hands / gauntlets.
- Public fountains, theater courts, masked salons.
- Values: civility, contract, elegance, diplomacy, favor-trading.

#### Federal Navy Ensign

Pennant-shaped blue ensign with:

- Dove holding olive branch.
- Canton with metallic clasped gauntlets.
- Gold star or navigational emblem.
- White and gold borders.

### Free State of Eldoria

A nation of outcasts, rebels, communes, defectors, and exiles who banded together to fight against tyranny and the conspiracy. It borders Solari to the west.

**Insignia:** A soaring silver falcon over a field of azure, clutching a broken chain in its talons. This represents freedom, independence, and the unyielding spirit of the Eldorian people.

**Known people:** Rebel Leader Marcus.

#### Political Structure

- Loose federation of communes and refuge-towns.
- Oaths are sworn to the **Free Charter**, not to lords.
- Supreme body: **Free Conclave**.
- Temporary executive: **Marshal of the Free**, elected for one year, non-consecutive.
- Judiciary: **Charter Courts** and citizen juries.
- Local rule: Communal Councils.

#### The Free Charter

Greek translation: **Η Ελεύθερη Χάρτα**.

It is a written constitution. Citizens swear loyalty to its laws, rights, and institutions, not to a person, lord, or dynasty.

**Core principles:**

- No hereditary rule.
- Public ledgers and open councils.
- Due process and citizen juries.
- Speech, assembly, petition.
- Free movement and sanctuary.
- No chattel slavery.
- Commons held for roads, beacons, levees, and granaries.
- Work-days or coin in lieu.
- Recallable officials.
- War powers limited by public vote.

**Oath of the Free:**

> I swear by falcon and broken chain to uphold the Free Charter, to answer summons of council and jury, to keep the commons in my care, and to yield my office at term. I bind myself to law, not to any lord.

**Motto:** Unbound, Unbowed. / Χωρίς Δεσμά, Αλύγιστοι.

#### Economy

**Major sectors:**

- Agri-commons: barley, beans, root crops, granaries.
- Timber and resin.
- Metal repair, refurbished arms, wagon and boat repair.
- Smuggling and courier routes.
- Printing and propaganda.
- Medicinals, field surgery, herbals.

**Currency:** Falcons silver, Links copper, Freedom Notes backed by timber and grain reserves.

#### Politics

**Factions:**

- Chainbreakers: radical revolution exporters.
- Steadiers: consolidate and build before escalation.
- Reconciliators: bargain with border nobles and allow amnesties.
- Sanctuary Bloc: focus on refugees, clinics, and education.

### Grand Duchy of Eridia

An autonomous elven once-great kingdom now in decline, but still wealthy in culture and resources. It borders Verosia by sea to the south and Solari by land to the south/southeast.

**Insignia:** A silver stag rearing on a forest green field, framed by oak leaves and acorns. The stag’s antlers are crowned with gold, symbolizing the Duchy’s connection to nature, prosperity, and nobility.

**Conspirators:**

- Minister Delacroix
- Captain Thorne

#### Culture

Eridia is elegant, formal, aristocratic, and tradition-bound. It has elven noble families, refined speech, artistic sophistication, and formal surnames tied to lineage and estates.

**Naming examples:**

- Male: Alaric Velmont, Lucian Draemir, Cedric Castillon, Victor Arundale, Dorian Faelbright.
- Female: Celeste Ashbourne, Genevieve Lorrain, Isolde Ravenshade, Catriona Duskveil.
- Surnames: Velmont, Arundale, Blackthorne, Ravencourt, Duskveil, Brightwater, Evermoor, Castillon, Faelbright, Wintervale.

#### Government

- Autonomous Grand Duchy of elven noble houses.
- Ruled by a Grand Duke or Grand Duchess, styled Warden of the Green Crown.
- High Council of Boughs includes great houses, guild-princes, and Oak Priory.
- Verdant Assizes and Oak Priory oversee forest law, rivers, tithes, and sacred groves.

#### Economy

**Sectors:**

- Timber and ship-masts: tallheart oak, white elm spars, pitch.
- Silver and moon-ore.
- Glass, crystal, lenses, luthier arts.
- Herbal lore, tinctures, perfumes, fungi.
- River trade, docks, mills, barge building.

**Currency:** Stagleaf silver, Acorn copper, Crown Rings bonds.

#### Why Eridia is in Decline

- Debt spiral from selling timber and silver forward.
- Aging levees, canals, mills, and river infrastructure.
- Solari pressure, price-fixing, and proxy concessions.
- Overcutting of ancient oak stands and poaching.
- Mine flooding and declining silver yields.
- High Houses resist reform.
- Temple–Guild tension causes policy paralysis.
- Brain drain of glassmakers, luthiers, engineers, and young artisans to Orlais and Verosia.
- Underpaid Greenwardens and thin military coverage.
- Arcanic dependence on foreign mages and enchantments.

**One-liner:** Eridia is not dead; it is bleeding slowly from debt, neglect, and manipulation.

### Principality of Valoria

A wealthy and influential country placed between Drakonia, Solari, Eldoria, and Orlais. It borders Zephyr to the north. It is home to powerful nobles, art, beauty, vibrant culture, and Renaissance-style political intrigue.

**Insignia:** A golden rose entwined with a silver rapier on a field of royal purple. The rose symbolizes beauty and grace, while the rapier represents precision and cunning.

**Conspirators:**

- Lady Fiona
- Lord Granger

Valoria has medieval / Renaissance Italian characteristics:

- City-states and signorie.
- Noble families, bankers, guilds, artists, and condottieri.
- Music, theater, frescoes, salons, courtly intrigue.
- Elegant, melodic names.

## Nexus Souls, Guardians, and the Sundering

### Nexus Souls

Nexus Souls are mystical fragments tied to divine or cosmic power. They are not stones, objects, or fuel. They cannot simply be used as batteries or immortality engines.

Verosia historically pursued the study of Nexus Souls. This caused conflict with Solari and contributed to the Verosia–Solari war and the catastrophe surrounding the Wound.

### Nexus Tracker

A dangerous relic tied to Nexus Souls. It can track or interact with Nexus Soul-related phenomena, bloodlines, or knowledge. It was eventually hidden away.

### Crystal Compass

A mystical artifact that reveals hidden paths, destinies, or sources of power.

### Ancient Book

A tome of cosmic and forbidden knowledge, said to contain truths not meant for mortals.

## The Guardians

The original Guardians were an ancient order charged with protecting, hiding, and preserving knowledge related to Nexus Souls and dangerous cosmic truths.

They eventually suffered a schism known as **The Sundering of the Guardians**.

### Prime Seal of the Guardians

**Core geometry:**

- Outer ring: the Ring of Worlds / multiverse.
- Three interlocked crescents: the three moons Lunareth, Vaelun, Seren and the Guardian charges: watch, ward, withhold.
- Central teardrop/flame: a Nexus Soul.
- Four tiny gaps in the ring: Gates / portals / planes the Guardians keep sealed.

**Meaning:** Protect the soul at the center; the moons bind it; the ring limits power; the gates represent restraint.

**Palette:** stone silver, deep indigo, ember-gold.

---

## The Sundering of the Guardians

The schism was not the original cause of the Verosia–Solari war; rather, the war and the schism occurred concurrently, and the schism intensified after the Wound happened.

The war involved Verosia and Solari struggling over Nexus knowledge and the Tracker items. The Wound intensified the ideological split.

### Core divide

- **Preservationists**: rejected use of Nexus Souls and chose secrecy, restraint, and protection.
- **Interventionists**: believed Nexus Souls and Guardian knowledge should be actively used to shape events and prevent catastrophe.

## The Shrouded Path

The Shrouded Path is the secret group that inherited the Guardians’ mission after the Sundering. They are the Preservationist successor faction.

### Identity

- Successor to the Guardians.
- Protect Nexus Souls and related knowledge.
- Reject overt interference.
- Use secrecy, riddles, archives, tests, and indirect guidance.
- Operate from the Athum Spires in Zephyr.

### Location

The Shrouded Path moved east to the **Athum Spires** in the **Sultanate of Zephyr**, rebuilding the Guardian mission in secrecy over several centuries.

### Mission

- Preserve Guardian knowledge.
- Protect Nexus Souls.
- Prevent misuse of the Nexus Tracker and memory-transfer lore.
- Test mortals rather than openly command them.
- Intervene minimally.

### Structure

- High Custodian.
- Circle of Shadows.
- Keepers and Initiates.

### Relationships

- Oppose Interventionists.
- Would not have helped Vaelith.
- Obstruct or misdirect Arcturus when possible.
- Treat mortals with caution.

### Shrouded Path Sigil

A hooded figure, crescent moons, and a spiraling/shrouded path motif. Visual style uses deep blues, indigo, silver, and lunar mystery.

## Interventionists / Rogue Faction

The Interventionists are the rogue faction that split from the Guardians. They believed in direct action and using forbidden Guardian knowledge to intervene in the world.

### Relationship to Arcturus

Arcturus could not have acted alone. He likely encountered a former Guardian, specifically an **Interventionist survivor**, who helped him understand or exploit Guardian/Nexus knowledge.

### Vaelith

Vaelith is a former Guardian and Interventionist survivor. His survival/immortality mechanism is unresolved and should not rely on using Nexus Souls as fuel. He also lost access to the Nexus Tracker items, and the Shrouded Path would not have helped him.

### Interventionist Sigil: The Red Aperture

**Design:**

- Fractured outer ring inherited from the Guardians’ Prime Seal.
- A deliberate breach in the ring.
- Three crescents tilted into a spearhead/arrow.
- Central Nexus Soul teardrop pierced or touched by a crimson rift-stroke.
- Crimson marks represent transgressive intervention.

**Meaning:** The seal can be opened when necessary. The world must be acted upon, not merely watched.

## The Wound

The Wound is a dangerous geographic barrier and major hazard on the route toward the Athum Spires.

### Key lore

- It emerged after the apocalyptic destruction of the sphere.
- It became prominent during the Verosia–Solari war and the Sundering of the Guardians.
- Verosia saw it as a perilous choke point that hindered pursuit when Sultanate-aligned spies stole the Nexus Tracker.
- Solari treated it pragmatically as an obstacle/waypoint.

### Nexus Tracker Theft Route

Verosian scholar’s home → Dead Trees Forest hideout → armor swap from Verosian to Solari armor → attempted Wound crossing → intended destination Athum Spires in Zephyr.

---

## Dead Trees Forest

The Dead Trees in the south are not truly dead. They are sustained by lingering magic left behind after ancient catastrophes.

### Tone and character

- Similar to the Svalich Woods of Barovia.
- The sun does not reach the deeper parts; lingering magic does.
- Sane and simple people avoid it.
- Desperate people and fugitives risk entering.
- The deeper one goes, the more dangerous and shifting the environment becomes.
- Legends say a covenant of hags rules the Verosian part of the forest.
- The chance of running into the hags feels more plausible with every step deeper.

---

## Arcturus and the Sphere

After the apocalyptic destruction of the sphere and creation of the Wound, Arcturus almost immediately found and recovered the sphere. The remaining Verosian army could not find traces of it and returned to their kingdom.

### Myrren D&D Effect Explained

“Once per long rest, treat a d20 roll you make as if it were a 10 after seeing the roll” means:

- Roll first.
- After seeing the die, but before the DM declares success/failure, you may replace the natural d20 result with 10.
- Add modifiers normally.
- It cannot create a critical hit because it is not a natural 20.
- It can rescue a very low roll, including a natural 1 if the DM allows.

## Regional Weekday Patrons

In Elidora, weekday patrons are not globally uniform. A weekday’s patron is the archetype, deity, office, ancestor, or celestial force that a culture ties that day to. It shapes rites, taboos, work, legal customs, and omens.

Different kingdoms can share a seven-day structure while interpreting the days through local culture.

## Elidora Pantheon and Cosmic Themes

The gods of Elidora guide, deceive, and war with one another. Divine influence echoes across the mortal plane.

### Pantheon categories

- Current gods: light, war, nature, magic, death, trickery, knowledge, protection, chaos, fate.
- Dead gods: forgotten deities of creation, shadow, ambition, storms, secrets.
- Cosmic beings: truths beyond divinity, including a mysterious god of Cosmic Truth worshipped in whispers by those seeking forbidden knowledge.

---

## Ages of Elidora

1. **Age of Dawning** — gods walked freely and shaped the lands.
2. **Age of Empires** — mortal kingdoms rose and fought wars of conquest.
3. **Age of Shadows** — rebellions, betrayals, and dynastic collapses.
4. **Age of Balance** — current age, where diplomacy and conspiracy are as deadly as steel.

---

## Key Campaign Themes

- Political intrigue, betrayal, and conspiracies.
- Balance between faith, magic, ambition, and state power.
- Forbidden knowledge and the danger of Nexus Souls.
- Secret factions such as the Shrouded Path and Interventionists.
- War, propaganda, and proxy conflict between Verosia and Solari.
- National identities expressed through flags, sigils, calendars, and local patron systems.
- Declining old powers, rising rebel states, and contested trade routes.
