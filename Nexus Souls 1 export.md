# Elidora Campaign Canon Summary

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Elidora** is a high-fantasy political intrigue campaign set on a small continent of rival nations, ancient magic, divine mysteries, and hidden conspiracies. The central tension revolves around the **Kingdom of Verosia**, the **Empire of Solari**, the forbidden legacy of the **Nexus Souls**, and the catastrophic weapon known as the **Sphere of Oblivion**.

The campaign themes are:

- Political intrigue
- War and manipulation
- Forbidden magical research
- Divine and cosmic secrets
- National guilt and historical coverups
- Tragic villains
- Moral ambiguity
- The consequences of power used without wisdom

At the heart of the campaign is the question:

**What happens when nations bury their sins, but the survivors do not?**

---

1. **Kingdom of Verosia**
2. **Empire of Solari**
3. **Dominion of Arathor**
4. **Republic of Calandria**
5. **Confederation of Drakonia**
6. **Principality of Valoria**
7. **Federation of Orlais**

9. **Grand Duchy of Eridia**
10. **Free State of Eldoria**

These nations exist in a fragile balance of diplomacy, rivalry, trade, espionage, and military tension.

## 3. Kingdom of Verosia

### Summary

The **Kingdom of Verosia** is a powerful monarchy ruled by **King Alistair of House Velanor**. It is known for chivalry, arcane scholarship, faith, knightly orders, and a strong ruling council known as the **Council of the Crown**.

Verosia once pursued dangerous research into the **Nexus Souls**, but later abandoned that path and chose a more peaceful and diplomatic direction. However, its past crimes and secrets continue to haunt it.

### Ruler

**King Alistair of House Velanor**

- A wise but cautious monarch.
- Desires peace and stability.
- Relies heavily on the Council of the Crown.
- His flaw is that he can be too trusting of advisors and too slow to act decisively.

### House Velanor

- Ancient royal house of Verosia.
- Symbol: **Golden griffon on a deep blue field**.
- Motto: **Strength Through Unity**.
- Associated with honor, justice, leadership, and the protection of Verosia.

### Verosian Identity

Verosia presents itself as noble, lawful, diplomatic, and protective. However, beneath that ideal image lies a buried history of forbidden research, political manipulation, and guilt surrounding the creation and use of the Sphere of Oblivion.

## 8. The Empire of Solari

### Summary

The **Empire of Solari** is a powerful, disciplined, expansionist empire. It is militaristic, politically ambitious, and willing to use covert manipulation to weaken rivals.

### Solarian Army

A disciplined and heavily organized imperial force known for strict hierarchy, radiant banners, siegecraft, and overwhelming battlefield coordination.

### Solari Officials

Short description:

**Ambitious imperial administrators who cloak expansionist policy in diplomacy, law, and divine rhetoric.**

### Relationship with Verosia

Solari and Verosia are rivals. Solari fears or exploits Verosia’s past connection to Nexus Soul research and the Sphere of Oblivion. Solari also becomes a tool in Arcturus’s revenge, though they do not truly understand who he is.

## 17. Nexus Souls / Pillars

### Definition

**Nexus Souls**, also called **Pillars**, are souls with immense magical energy and power. They are rare individuals whose souls can fuel powerful artifacts, rituals, and cosmic mechanisms.

### Importance

- They are tied to ancient magical research.
- They can power the Sphere of Oblivion.
- Their fate after being consumed is unknown.
- No one knows whether they continue to exist, are destroyed, or are displaced into another reality.

### Moral Question

The use of Nexus Souls raises the central moral horror of the campaign:

**Is power worth using if it consumes people whose fate cannot be known?**

## 18. The Nexus Tracker

The **Nexus Tracker** is a set of three items that work together to locate Nexus Souls.

### Components

1. **Crystal Compass**
   - Attuned to Nexus Souls.
   - Points toward the nearest Nexus Soul.
   - Powered by the energy of the souls themselves.

2. **Book with Empty Pages**
   - Enchanted to record the names and locations of Nexus Souls when discovered.
   - Can only be activated by someone who possesses the memories necessary to reveal the locations.
   - Arielle is the link to the Nexus Souls’ memories.

3. **Vial of Liquid**
   - Rare magical ingredient required for the book to record names and locations.
   - May come from a mystical flower, rare mineral, volcano heart, ocean depths, hidden valley, or dangerous cave system.
   - Obtaining it requires a dangerous quest.

### Arielle

Arielle possesses the link to the Nexus Souls’ memories. The heroes do not have such powers themselves.

### Arcturus and the Tracker

Arcturus cares about retrieving and controlling the book and related artifacts because they can lead to Nexus Souls, which can fuel the Sphere of Oblivion.

## 21. The Sphere of Oblivion

### Summary

The **Sphere of Oblivion** is a semi-sentient magical weapon powered by Nexus Souls, also called Pillars. It is one of the most dangerous artifacts in Elidora.

### Appearance

The Sphere of Oblivion appears as a smooth black orb about the size of a human head. Its surface drinks in light rather than reflecting it. Within it swirl faint silver and violet patterns like cracks in reality. It feels both solid and fluid, like stone and water at the same time.

It hums faintly near magic and seems to possess a quiet hunger.

### Nature

- Semi-sentient.
- Not loyal to any wielder.
- Hungers for Nexus Souls.
- Whispers subtly to those near it.
- Cannot easily be destroyed.
- Can be contained, hidden, or perhaps starved.

### Power

The Sphere does not simply destroy. It **pushes things out of existence**.

It can:

- Displace matter out of space and time.
- Push forests, villages, people, animals, and terrain into another plane.
- Bring in matter or energy from another reality in exchange.
- Replace land with sea, unstable magic, or alien matter.
- Consume Nexus Souls as fuel.
- Create reality scars.

### Soul Fuel

The Sphere is powered by Nexus Souls. No one knows what happens to these souls after use.

Possibilities include:

- They are destroyed.
- They are trapped.
- They are displaced.
- They continue to exist in torment.
- They become part of the Sphere’s hunger.
- They are scattered into unstable planes.

### Historical Use

The Sphere was used once during the catastrophic conflict between Verosia and Solari.

Its activation created **The Wound**.

## 22. The Catastrophe of the Sphere

A thousand years ago, during conflict between Verosia and Solari, the Sphere of Oblivion was unleashed in the borderlands.

The affected region included:

- Villages
- Forests
- Valleys
- Battlefields
- Animals
- Civilians
- Soldiers
- Neutral border communities

The Sphere did not merely destroy them. It displaced them into another plane made of unstable magical energy.

In exchange, the original land was replaced by sea, unstable magic, and alien terrain.

This created **The Wound**.

## 26. The Dead Trees Forest

### Summary

The **Dead Trees** are a forest near the southern part of The Wound.

Despite the name, the trees are not truly dead. They are sustained by the lingering magic left behind after the Sphere’s destruction.

### Tone

The Dead Trees should feel like the **Svalich Woods of Barovia**:

- Oppressive
- Dark
- Dangerous
- Avoided by sane people
- Entered by desperate people
- Full of shifting paths and dread
- More dangerous the deeper one travels

### Light and Magic

The sun does not reach the deeper parts of the forest. Where sunlight fails, lingering unstable magic provides eerie illumination.

### Who Enters

Sane and simple people avoid the forest.

Those who enter are usually:

- Desperate fugitives
- People on the run
- Outlaws
- Deserters
- Refugees
- Hunters of forbidden knowledge
- People with no better choice

### Progression of Danger

#### Outskirts

- Sparse light.
- Twisted trees.
- Heavy silence.
- Few animals.
- Oppressive but survivable.

#### Deeper Woods

- Air thickens.
- Ozone and iron smell.
- Glowing fungi and moss.
- Paths shift when unobserved.
- The environment becomes unreliable.

#### Heart of the Forest

- Reality frays.
- Time feels stretched.
- Groves vanish and reappear.
- Whispers come from nowhere.
- The chance of encountering hags becomes more plausible with every step.

### The Hag Covenant

Legend says a covenant of hags rules the Verosian part of the forest.

They may:

- Feed on memories and souls.
- Bargain with desperate travelers.
- Know secrets of The Wound.
- Hear echoes from the displaced plane.
- Use the trees as anchors of power.

The deeper one goes, the more likely the hags become.

## 27. Arcturus: True Backstory

### Identity

Arcturus was originally a human from a neutral border village between Verosia and Solari.

He was not Verosian.
He was not Solarian.
He was not a soldier.
He was not a chosen one.
He was not special.

He was simply a man living in a village caught between two powers.

### The Village

His village stood in the borderlands where the Sphere of Oblivion was unleashed.

The village was neutral and belonged to neither Verosia nor Solari. Its people were ordinary civilians: farmers, families, merchants, and simple folk.

When the Sphere was activated, the village and surrounding lands were displaced.

### Survival

Arcturus survived by tragic accident.

He was caught at the edge of the catastrophe, where the Sphere’s displacement did not fully take him. He was pushed out of time, but not out of the physical plane.

He continued to exist after the event, aging unnaturally slowly or being left outside the normal flow of time.

His survival gave him no special powers and no chosen destiny. It was not a gift.

It was a curse.

### What He Lost

He lost:

- His family
- His village
- His homeland
- His people
- His place in history
- His sense of belonging
- His natural life

He became a living remnant of an atrocity that both nations tried to forget.

### Why He Hates Verosia

Arcturus blames Verosia for the forbidden Nexus Soul research and the creation or use of the Sphere.

He also despises Solari, but uses them as a tool.

His hatred of Verosia is sharper because he sees Verosia as the nation that tampered with the power that destroyed his life.

### Relationship with Solari

Arcturus secretly works with Solari, but:

- He is not part of the Solari government.
- He does not reveal his true identity.
- He does not truly serve Solari.
- He uses Solari for revenge.
- Solari believes he is a useful hidden ally.

### Role in Verosia

Arcturus infiltrated Verosia’s power structure and became second-in-command to High Magus Zephyrion on the Council of the Crown.

He manipulates King Alistair and the council into believing Solari is the primary threat, while secretly shaping events for his own revenge.

### Villain Type

Arcturus is a tragic villain.

He is not evil because he wants power for its own sake. He is evil because grief, guilt, and rage have consumed him.

His tragedy is that he became the kind of monster who creates more victims like himself.

---

## 28. How Arcturus Survived

Arcturus survived the Sphere’s destruction because he was caught in an imperfect fracture of reality.

The Sphere displaced the land unevenly. Entire regions were pushed into the unstable magic plane, while other fragments were replaced by sea and magical energy.

Arcturus was caught at the seam.

He was not fully displaced.
He was not fully erased.
He was not saved.

He was left behind.

His body remained on the physical plane, but his existence was damaged by the temporal and planar fracture. Time no longer treated him normally. This is why he continued to exist for over a thousand years.

This does not give him special powers. It only makes him a survivor of something he should not have survived.

---

## 29. How Arcturus Found the Sphere

After the catastrophe, the Verosian army, or what remained of it, searched for traces of the Sphere but found nothing. The land was unstable, flooded, dangerous, and magically corrupted. The survivors retreated back to Verosia.

Arcturus, however, was still there.

Almost immediately after the destruction, he found the Sphere half-buried in the warped remains of the land. While the armies fled and history began to cover itself, Arcturus recovered the weapon.

To the world, the Sphere was lost or destroyed.

In truth, Arcturus had it.

He recovered it not as a conqueror, but as a broken survivor holding the object that had erased everything he loved.

---

## 30. Arcturus and the Sphere

Arcturus’s relationship with the Sphere is tragic and dangerous.

The Sphere is:

- The weapon that destroyed his life.
- The proof of Verosia’s sin.
- The only physical link to the catastrophe.
- The tool he may use to take revenge.
- The object he both hates and cannot abandon.

He may believe he controls it, but the Sphere is semi-sentient and may subtly influence him.

The question remains:

**Is Arcturus using the Sphere, or has the Sphere been using his grief for a thousand years?**

## 32. Solari Invasion Clues

There is increased Solari military activity near the Verosian border, including more patrols and troop movements.

This clue is easy to find.

### Border Sightings

A Verosian scout reports seeing a large, well-armed Solari army gathering just beyond the border, but cannot confirm numbers or intentions.

This clue is difficult to find.

### Operation Sunfire Intel

Verosian refugees who escaped a recent Solari conquest overheard Solari officers discussing a surprise attack on Verosia.

They called it **Operation Sunfire**.

They mentioned a date, but it was spoken in code and requires knowledge of Solari military tactics to decipher.

This clue is hidden and very hard to find.

### Slip of the Tongue Armory Door Sabotage

The heroes stumble upon a secret meeting between Solari military leaders and foreign fighters. One foreign fighter accidentally mentions a specific code phrase, revealing they are not merely defending Solari but actively participating in the invasion of Verosia.

This clue is hidden and very hard to find.

## 39. Core Campaign Summary

Elidora is a high-fantasy political intrigue campaign set on a continent of ten rival nations, where ancient magic, divine secrets, and buried atrocities shape the present. The Kingdom of Verosia seeks peace after abandoning forbidden Nexus Soul research, but its past returns through the Sphere of Oblivion, the Wound, and Archmage Arcturus, a tragic survivor of Verosia’s greatest sin. As Solari pressure rises, rebel movements stir, and cosmic truths emerge, the heroes must navigate deception, diplomacy, war, and the terrible cost of power.

---

## 40. Short Player-Facing Summary

Elidora is a continent on the edge of war, where ten nations compete through diplomacy, espionage, and magic. The Kingdom of Verosia stands at the center of a growing conspiracy involving Solari aggression, ancient artifacts, and forbidden research into powerful souls called Nexus Souls. Beneath the politics lies a deeper horror: a forgotten weapon once tore land, people, and history out of existence, leaving behind a cursed region called The Wound. Now the past is returning, and the heroes may be the only ones who can uncover the truth before nations fall.

---

## 41. DM-Facing Secret Summary

Arcturus is the hidden mastermind behind much of the campaign’s instability. He is a thousand-year-old human survivor of the Sphere of Oblivion catastrophe. His neutral border village was displaced into an unstable magic plane when the Sphere was used, creating The Wound. He survived by accident, pushed out of time but not out of the physical plane. He found and recovered the Sphere almost immediately after the disaster, while Verosian forces failed to locate it and retreated. He later infiltrated Verosia’s Council of the Crown and secretly works with Solari without revealing his true identity. His goal is not Solari victory, but revenge against Verosia.

## 42. Essential Canon Rules

1. Arcturus is human, not elf or immortal by nature.
2. Arcturus has no special chosen-one powers from the Sphere.
3. His survival is tragic, accidental, and cursed.
4. He was from a neutral border village, neither Verosian nor Solarian.
5. The Sphere of Oblivion is semi-sentient.
6. The Sphere is powered by Nexus Souls/Pillars.
7. The Sphere displaces matter, energy, and people out of space and time.
8. The Wound was created by the Sphere.
9. The displaced land went to a plane of unstable magic.
10. The Dead Trees are not truly dead; they are sustained by lingering magic.
11. The Dead Trees become more dangerous the deeper one travels.
12. A hag covenant is rumored to rule the Verosian part of the Dead Trees.
13. The Nexus Tracker locates Nexus Souls.
14. The book with empty pages records Nexus Soul names and locations when properly activated.
15. Arielle is the link to Nexus Soul memories.
16. Solari does not know Arcturus’s true identity.
17. Arcturus uses Solari but does not truly serve them.
18. Verosia’s past pursuit of Nexus Soul knowledge is central to the campaign.
19. The campaign tone is political, tragic, mysterious, and morally gray.
20. The central historical wound is both literal and symbolic.
