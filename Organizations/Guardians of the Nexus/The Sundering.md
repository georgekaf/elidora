# The Sundering of the Guardians

## Overview
The Sundering of the Guardians happened because at the time the Guardinas were investigating the Souls and how they affect the cosmos some of the magical ingredients couls only be found in Verosia's Fog Iron mountains. They asked for assistance for looking at them the then King agreed in exchange for something that could protect them. 

After months of deliberation and in the name of the pursuit of knowledge the guardians decided to build such a thing with some divine help from [[Thalgron, The Forge Father]]. For countless eons Thalgron and the other divines were at war with the old pantheon of Verosia, until Thalgron managed to create a device that could push antyhing from the current plane of existense. This led to win the war against them. Thalgron created the design for an infinitely "dumber" and simpler version of this device, on that could be used by and against mortals. For Thalgorn it was an experiment to see what can be done with it and to see if mortals posed any threat against the gods, he was mildly impressed that a small group of scholars and mages discovered what the nexus souls do, so he also saw it as a small reward to keep their thirst of knowledge alive, but for the Guardians it was a sign of divine help. 

Thus the sphere of oblivion was eventually created but with three failsafes. Anything pushed outside of existence will end up in Thalgron's domain for ...safekeeping (this was not made known to the Guardians) and there will be no distinction of "sides" (this was made know to the Guardians). If activated it would be used against everything and everyone. The last one was how this device was powered, the original could be powered by the gods themselves but this device could be powered by the nexus souls. Small anchors here and there that hold the nexus in its place and stable. Another failsafe that was incorporated in the design was that the souls could only power the shpere of oblivion if they were in a dormant state. Only then the sphere could absorb them. This orb needed at least 5-10 souls and a catalyst soul that would trigger the displacememt of matter. The device once created was powered up by Thalgron himself and only a catalyst soul was needed to activated.
## The Schism
Some saw Thalgron's intervention as a test for mankind and one that the guardian's failed. How much should we sacrifice and do in the pursuit of unlocking how the cosmos is working? For Thalgron it was just an experiment and a way to pass his time but the Guardians did not know that. Some argued that there was another way to uncover knowledge, some were just too impatient to explore other ways.

The sphere was eventually given to the kingdom of Verosia and they were allowed to continue their research.
As the war unfolded and the The wound happened, the once-united Guardians fractured even more and two rival factions were created:

### The Interventionists
- Believed the Nexus Souls should be used actively to shape the world for its betterment.
- Aligned with Verosia in hopes of securing stability through strength.
- Ultimately broken by the devastation of the Wound, which exposed the terrible cost of their philosophy.
 
### The Preservationists (The Shrouded Path)
- Recoiled in horror at the use of Nexus Souls as weapons.
- Condemned both Verosia and Solari for their recklessness.
- Fled into secrecy, rebuilding their mission in the **Athum Spires** of the Sultanate of Zephyr.
- Adopted a creed of silence, secrecy, and watchfulness.

## The Wound
The Sundering became irrevocable after the Wound was created. This event not only scarred the land but also revealed the destructive potential of the Nexus Souls. With trust shattered, reconciliation among the Guardians was impossible.

## Legacy
- The Interventionists were left scattered and diminished, their influence waning in the centuries that followed.  
- The Shrouded Path endured in the shadows, keeping to their secret charge and avoiding direct interference.  
- The Sundering remains a defining event, shaping the hidden conflicts and silent wars that continue in Elidora to this day.
