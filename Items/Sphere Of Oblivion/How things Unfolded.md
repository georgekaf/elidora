### ⚖️ 1. **The Misfired Annihilation**

The Sphere of Oblivion didn’t function perfectly when it was first used. The destructive surge struck the borderlands unevenly, and Arcturus happened to be caught on the _edge_ of the wave.

- Everyone around him was erased, but he was only “scorched” by the displacement effect.
- He lived, but forever marked by survivor’s guilt.
- His survival is meaningless chance — an accident of position, not destiny.

💀 Tragedy: He witnessed the horror firsthand and lives with the burden that it _should have been him too._

---

### 🌫️ 2. **The Shield of Cowardice**

Arcturus survived because he wasn’t brave — he ran.

- When the weapon was unleashed, he hid behind arcane wards, structures, or even _behind others_
- The wards or barriers absorbed most of the force, sparing him while others died.
- He knows his survival wasn’t noble or miraculous — just selfish instinct.

💀 Tragedy: He sees himself as a coward, unworthy of survival, which twists into hatred for Verosia (blaming them instead of himself).

---

### 🕯️ 3. **Survivor of Absence**

He was spared simply because he was _not quite there at the moment_.

- Perhaps he had collapsed from exhaustion, been knocked into a ravine, or trapped underground.
- The Sphere’s wave passed above him, erasing everything on the surface, but he remained unnoticed.
- When he emerged, he found an empty wasteland where his people once stood.

💀 Tragedy: His survival feels hollow, almost insulting — he lived not because of strength, but because he was out of sight, forgotten by fate.

---

### 🧩 4. **The Flawed Fuel**

The Sphere was powered by **Nexus Souls**, and during the event it briefly faltered.

- At the moment it struck his location, its power waned just enough to leave scraps of the world behind.
- He was among the fragments of reality left untouched.
- When the Sphere drew in more fuel, destruction resumed elsewhere — but his small patch was already “missed.”

💀 Tragedy: He knows he was spared by nothing more than a glitch in a weapon of horror. He sees no meaning in his survival, only resentment.

---

### 🥀 5. **The Bitter Irony**

Arcturus survived not because of magic, courage, or chance, but because he was **already condemned**.

- He had been shackled, exiled, or imprisoned underground by his peers when the Sphere struck.
- When he emerged, he discovered his captors and rivals had all been erased… and he alone remained.

💀 Tragedy: He was _meant_ to be gone, yet he endured while better men and women vanished. He hates Verosia because in his eyes, they punished him and then _left him to bear the scars of survival alone_.

Essentially:

When the Sphere of Oblivion was unleashed a thousand years ago, Arcturus was a young mage serving on the border between Verosia and Solari. He should have died with everyone else — but his survival was the result of _cruel coincidences layered together._

- **The Edge of the Wave (Misfired Annihilation):** He stood at the very edge of the blast. The full force tore through everything ahead of him, but he was struck by only a _fractured ripple_ of the weapon’s power. His body was scarred by reality’s unraveling, but he endured.
- **The Coward’s Shelter (Shield of Cowardice):** In the chaos, he threw up wards of protection and ducked behind others. The wards crumbled instantly, and the soldiers shielding him were annihilated, but their last moments spared him the first strike. He lived with the memory that he survived because others died in front of him.
- **The Hidden Refuge (Survivor of Absence):** Thrown into a collapsed ravine during the blast, he lay unconscious underground. The Sphere’s destruction passed over him, erasing everything on the surface. When he awoke and clawed his way out, the world he knew was _gone_.
- **The Flawed Weapon (The Flawed Fuel):** For a heartbeat, the Sphere faltered, its hunger for Nexus Souls momentarily unsated. Arcturus’ small patch of reality remained untouched while the blast resumed elsewhere. This "glitch" mocked him — he was not chosen, merely _skipped_.
- **The Bitter Irony (Condemned Survivor):** Before the catastrophe, Arcturus had been under suspicion of treachery by his Verosian peers — shackled, ridiculed, and thrown aside during a skirmish. The irony is unbearable: those who condemned him were obliterated, while he, the one meant to be forgotten, endured.
### ⚖️ **Why This Matters**

Arcturus’ survival is not clean, not noble, not magical — it’s _messy, unfair, and cruel_. He is not alive because of strength or destiny, but because fate tripped over itself in a thousand small ways.

To him, this survival is **not a blessing, but a curse.**

- He feels like a _cosmic mistake_, existing when he should not.
- He bears _survivor’s guilt_, twisted into hatred for Verosia — the nation he blames for creating, fueling, and unleashing the Nexus-fueled weapon.
- He resents the gods themselves, for letting him linger while everyone else was erased.
- His tragedy is that he has lived for a thousand years, not out of choice, but because the Sphere pushed him “out of time,” tethering him unnaturally to existence.


a man who survived not through destiny, but through a cruel series of accidents. He is broken, bitter, and full of rage at the injustice of his survival.

He wasn’t even part of the political game that unleashed the Sphere of Oblivion. He was just an ordinary man, caught between empires, living in a borderland village that neither Verosia nor Solari truly claimed or protected. That makes his survival and his hatred even more compelling, because:

- **He lost everything** — his home, family, and community were erased, not because of any fault of their own, but because the great powers treated their lives as expendable in their endless rivalry.
- **He was no one important** — not a soldier, not a leader, not a hero. Just a man who lived in the wrong place at the wrong time.
- **He has no nation** — his village’s identity was neutral, caught between borders. Now, he blames _both_ Verosia and Solari, though his vengeance focuses on Verosia, whom he holds more responsible for experimenting with Nexus knowledge.
- **His existence is a scar** — he is the living memory of a crime everyone else has tried to erase from history. Both Verosia and Solari would rather forget the border destruction — but Arcturus cannot.

### 🩸 **How He Survived**

Here’s how we can fold the tragedy into his village backstory:

When the Sphere was unleashed, Arcturus’ village was erased instantly. He was at the _edge of the blast_, tending fields near the forest line. He was thrown into a collapsed root cave as the world around him was shredded from reality. The destruction washed over his village like a tide — but skipped over the patch of ground where he lay buried.

When he clawed his way out days later, the village was _gone_. Not ruined. Not burned. **Gone.** Where his home, neighbors, and family once stood, there was only barren emptiness — soil and stone that did not belong to this world.

---

### ⚖️ **Why This Matters to His Character**

- **No national loyalty:** He wasn’t Verosian or Solarian. He was nothing to them, and they proved it. This fuels his bitterness — to him, nations are just machines of power that grind ordinary people into dust.
- **Hatred of Verosia:** Verosia, however, was the one experimenting with Nexus knowledge, creating tools like the Sphere of Oblivion. He lays the ultimate blame on them.
- **Tragic bitterness:** He wasn’t spared because of worth or power. He was spared by accident. That “cosmic mistake” kept him alive — a man who should have been gone with the rest.
- **His revenge:** To him, destroying Verosia isn’t just vengeance. It’s justice for all the innocents erased by powers who never even acknowledged their existence.

---

👉 This makes Arcturus the **perfect tragic villain**:  
He’s not a power-hungry schemer by nature, nor a man born to be special. He’s a victim, turned bitter, who _chooses_ to become a monster because the world left him no other meaning.

When the Sphere of Oblivion unleashed its cataclysm, the land shattered and the Wound was born. Amidst the chaos, armies scattered and survivors fled, believing nothing could have endured. Yet in the silence after the destruction, Arcturus remained—displaced in time but not erased. In the smoking craters and warped terrain of his homeland, he stumbled upon the Sphere almost immediately, half-buried among the wreckage. To others, it had vanished into myth, thought lost to the collapsing planes. The Verosian forces, broken and desperate to retreat, could not linger in the unstable, hostile land. They found no trace and withdrew, leaving the artifact forgotten.

But Arcturus did not leave. With nowhere else to turn, he recovered the weapon, not out of design but desperation—a lone survivor clutching the very thing that had destroyed everything he once loved.
