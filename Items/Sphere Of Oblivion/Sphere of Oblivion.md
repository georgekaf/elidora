
The **Sphere of Oblivion** is a semi-sentient magical weapon of catastrophic design, created during the age when kingdoms sought to harness the power of the **Nexus Souls**. At first glance, it resembles a smooth black orb, about the size of a man’s head, with a glasslike surface that seems to drink in light rather than reflect it. Within its depths swirl faint patterns of silver and violet energy, like cracks in reality itself, shifting as though the orb contains an endless void.

The Sphere is not truly solid—its surface shimmers and distorts, and touching it feels like pressing against both stone and water. It hums with an almost inaudible resonance, vibrating in tune with nearby sources of magic, and whispers of half-heard voices sometimes bleed into the minds of those who linger near it.

**Function and Power:**

- Fueled by **Nexus Souls** (souls with immense magical energy), it consumes them as fuel, leaving the ultimate fate of these souls unknown.
    
- Its power is to **“push” existence away**—erasing matter, energy, or even living beings from the world by displacing them outside of time and space. What is erased may be replaced by foreign energy or matter from unstable planes, causing unpredictable devastation.
    
- When unleashed, it manifests as concentric ripples of distortion, bending light and sound before violently snapping reality apart.
    

**Nature:**

- The Sphere is **semi-sentient**. It does not speak, but it _hungers_. Those who bear it long enough often feel compelled to use it, as though it projects a quiet will to fulfill its purpose—obliteration.
    
- It is not loyal to any wielder, and it cannot be truly destroyed, only contained or hidden.
    

**Historical Infamy:**

- A thousand years ago, during a brutal border conflict, the Sphere was unleashed near the lands between Verosia and Solari. Entire villages, forests, and battlefields were consumed, torn out of existence and replaced by unstable seas of magical energy. This wound in the world never healed, leaving a scar of chaos and raw magic.
    
- Among the survivors was **Arcturus**, who bore witness to the devastation. Though he survived physically, time itself seemed to flow strangely around him—trapping him in existence long beyond his natural span, leaving him as a tragic relic of an atrocity.
