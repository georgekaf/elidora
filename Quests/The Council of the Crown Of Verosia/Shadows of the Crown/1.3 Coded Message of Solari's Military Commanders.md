[[Lord Valerian - Master of Tactics]] - Is leading the kingdom's armies in a critical battle and needs the heroes to act as a special forces unit, completing strategic objectives behind enemy lines.

As the heroes carry out their special forces missions behind enemy lines, they come across a hidden cache of documents belonging to [[1.1 Solari's Emblem on Rebel Weapons]]'s military commanders. One of these documents catches their eye - it is a coded message that appears to be discussing the funding of rebel groups in Verosia.

Realizing the importance of this message, the heroes carefully study the document and try to decipher the code. It quickly becomes apparent that the cipher is complex and requires specialized knowledge to crack. Fortunately, the heroes have a member skilled in cryptography or intelligence gathering, and they are able to make progress on breaking the code.

After hours of intense work, the heroes finally manage to crack the cipher and translate the message. To their shock, the message confirms their suspicions - [[The Empire of Solari]]'s military commanders have been involved in funding the rebel groups in [[The Kingdom of Verosia]]. The message contains detailed information on the transactions and the shell companies used to transfer the money, as well as the involvement of [[Merchant Guildmaster Renard]] of [[The Republic of Calandria]].

The heroes realize that this evidence could be critical in proving the guilt of the high-ranking officials in Verosia who received the money, and stopping them from carrying out their plan. With the coded message in hand, the heroes continue their missions, hoping to use this information to make a decisive impact on the battlefield and ultimately bring an end to the conflict.

Here are some possible special forces missions for the heroes to undertake behind enemy lines in a D&D campaign:

Sabotage enemy supply lines: The heroes must sneak into an enemy encampment or convoy and destroy supplies or steal valuable resources, hindering their ability to wage war.

Rescue hostages: The heroes must infiltrate an enemy stronghold or prison camp to rescue prisoners of war or important hostages, potentially gaining valuable intel or allies in the process.

Assassination: The heroes are tasked with taking out a high-value target, such as an enemy general or key strategist, potentially disrupting enemy plans and morale.

Reconnaissance: The heroes must gather intelligence on enemy troop movements, fortifications, and other key strategic information, potentially allowing their own forces to plan and execute successful attacks.

Demolition: The heroes must sneak into an enemy facility or fortification and destroy critical infrastructure or weapons, potentially weakening the enemy's ability to wage war.

Espionage: The heroes must infiltrate an enemy stronghold or government building to gather sensitive information, such as battle plans, political alliances, or technological advancements.

Raid: The heroes must lead a surprise attack on an enemy outpost or patrol, potentially capturing prisoners or valuable resources while simultaneously weakening enemy morale.

Infiltration: The heroes must disguise themselves as enemy soldiers or civilians and blend in behind enemy lines, potentially gaining access to sensitive information or sabotaging enemy plans from within.

Extraction: The heroes must locate and extract friendly agents or operatives who are behind enemy lines, potentially gaining valuable intel or allies in the process.

Ambush: The heroes must set up an ambush for an enemy convoy or patrol, potentially inflicting significant casualties and disrupting enemy plans.
